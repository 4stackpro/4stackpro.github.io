Meet Amanda Staveley: The financial fixer on the verge of a £300m deal for Newcastle

In early 2009 she was described as ‘the beautiful woman who has shaken up the beautiful game’ – the financial fixer who helped Middle Eastern multi-millionaires purchase football clubs.

Eleven years later, Amanda Staveley is still dabbling in the art of takeovers, and perhaps coming towards the end of a pursuit that has left Newcastle United fans exhausted.

The sale of this North East institution is one which has been on, off, then on again, then off again.

Amanda Staveley appears to finally be closing in on a £300m takeover deal for Newcastle

But should Mike Ashley eventually sign on that dotted line, take his £300million and Sports Direct signage with him, those supporters will tell you it was all worth it.

Staveley could be about to become the new First Lady of Football. To understand who she is and what she stands for, one quote from the 2000s stands out.

‘It is not about money,’ the now 47-year-old said at the time. ‘It wouldn’t matter if I was making £8m or £200m. I just want to go to bed at night and say I’ve done a good job.’

If £8m is indeed peanuts to her, then Newcastle’s supporters desperate for success would hope that means she would be willing to invest significantly more in the transfer market.

She is thought to have a personal fortune of £115m-plus, and is no stranger to the limelight.

Staveley was in a relationship with Prince Andrew, the Duke of York, and she avoided a life of intense tabloid interest by apparently turning down a marriage proposal in 2003.

The British businesswoman has launched a new £300million bid for the North East giants

Staveley has strong contacts in the Middle East and has always been interested in sport

Newcastle owner Mike Ashley is ready to sell to Staveley and her firm PCP Capital Partners

In 2011 she married Mehrdad Ghodoussi, a handsome Iranian who worked with her at her company, in a lavish ceremony in West Wycombe Park, Buckinghamshire. Guests included the artist Tracey Emin and the bride’s wedding dress was designed by Sarah Burton, who was responsible for Kate Middleton’s gown at her own royal wedding.

The couple have a child together – Alexander, nicknamed Lexi, born in 2014. He was born prematurely after Staveley went into labour in a business meeting but is now a healthy little boy.

So why football? Why a business where each and every decision she makes would be scrutinised by millions of fans and reporters and pundits alike?

Staveley is not new to sport, having been well involved in the horse racing world. Like football, their owners have rather large bank balances.

As an ambitious 20-something, she took out a £180,000 loan to buy a restaurant named Stocks near Cambridge and Newmarket racecourse. It was regularly visited by trainers and owners, giving her an opportunity to make the contacts she craved.

Staveley poses with her husband Mehrdad Ghodoussi at The Old Vic Bicentenary Ball in 2013

Her entrepreneurial career was on its way.

Staveley first met Prince Andrew in the United Arab Emirates during a visit as part of his role as a trade ambassador, in 2001. She had just ended a two-year relationship with millionaire venture capitalist Mark Horrocks, then 39.

Her relationship with the Prince blossomed and was mutually beneficial in many ways.

As trade envoy, Prince Andrew could open doors to contacts. Meanwhile, he enjoyed introducing people to his new ‘female friend’, whose glamour was equalled by her charm and intelligence – an enviable asset for the Prince, who was often considered socially rather gauche. Friends noted how she called him ‘Babe’.

She became a regular visitor to Buckingham Palace, Sandringham and Royal Lodge, Andrew’s home in Windsor Great Park, which she helped him decorate. She was even introduced to Princesses Beatrice and Eugenie, who were said to have approved of her.

Staveley played a key role in Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed al-Nahyan buying Manchester City. She is pictured in 2008 with Ali Jassim, who was his adviser at the time

Staveley speaks at the Harper’s Bazaar Women Of The Year Awards in London in 2009

In 2003, the Prince popped the question. But Staveley struggled to envisage herself idling away her hours with official duties and being unable to conduct the business she loves.

‘Andrew’s a lovely man and I still care for him a great deal,’ she told The Daily Mail a few years ago. ‘But if I’d married him, my independence would have disappeared.’

It was a decision that disappointed her parents, particularly her mother, who Staveley later said took three years to get over it. She once admitted: ‘I had a bit of a chip on my shoulder about being a girl in a family where the boy inherits. My mother and father always told me that my job was to marry well.’

Marrying into the Royal Family would have curtailed her commercial ambitions, too. She had considered acquiring a stake in Spearmint Rhino, the chain of lap-dancing clubs, but declined because such a seedy investment would have been seen as inappropriate for a princess.

And so she turned to the Middle East to use her skills as a negotiator and broker deals to amass a princely sum.

Staveley attends ‘The Radical Eye’ dinner in aid of the Elton John Aids Foundation in 2016

In 2005, she set up the Dubai-based private equity company PCP Capital Partners, which soon secured international deals for clients in Qatar and the UAE.

The company’s role is varied. Sometimes they lobby on political issues; occasionally they simply bring powerful people together or act as intermediaries in complex negotiations. Her contacts book is, without doubt, formidable.

She had used her former Mayfair mews house to entertain the fashion designer Amanda Wakeley and the billionaire property-owning Duke of Westminster.

In 2008, at the age of 35, Staveley was involved in the takeover of Manchester City by Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed al-Nahyan. That reportedly saw her take home £10m and she was labelled by some newspapers as ‘the new Queen of British football’. Chelsea director Marina Granovskaia may now have some competition once more.

Staveley also came close to joining the Liverpool board when Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum of Dubai was linked with buying the Merseyside club.

Plus, she has been described as quite the sportswoman, liking showjumping and athletics. At the age of 14, she is said to have clocked 12.6 seconds for the 100m, and only a snapped Achilles tendon stopped her from pursuing a career in that side of sport more.

Before opening Stocks, she had won a place to study modern languages in Cambridge and worked as a part-time model for additional money.

She was unfortunately forced to abandon the degree after being left in hospital suffering from stress following the death of her grandfather.

So after all of this, it soon becomes apparent that Staveley is something of a workaholic.

Staveley poses in her London home as part of an interview with the Mail in March 2017

Weeks after helping the City takeover in 2008, she brokered a deal that saw billions of pounds of Middle Eastern money pumped into Barclays. Her slice of the pie from that deal was £40m for her firm, with Staveley reportedly keeping a significant sum for herself.

With a grand Regency townhouse in Park Lane, London, and a home in Dubai, it really is some life she leads.

So we know why sport and football, but why Newcastle? Staveley may have answered that herself in 2002: ‘If I’m shown a good idea, I can’t help but look.’

Newcastle, with its global appeal and room to grow, represents a challenge but also an opportunity. Ashley bought the club for £134m in 2007. In the past he has demanded £400m, then that figured appeared to drop to £340m, and now £300m is being discussed.

We have been here before, of course, but Staveley has never been closer to a done deal. Now we wait

英超傳紐卡素同意賣盤予沙特王室 Kardashian之友成關鍵人物

紐卡素放盤已久，但賣價一直都未合「孤寒班主」Mike Ashley心水，故球迷一直只能乾等。雖然英超現時因新冠肺炎停擺，但紐卡素賣盤一事卻有突破，英媒指Ashley已同意以約3億英鎊，將「喜鵲」賣給沙特阿拉伯王室的投資基金，更爆出交易成功全靠美國名媛Kim Kardashian的朋友Carla DiBello。

Mike Ashley 2007年入主，但因「孤寒」成性於紐卡素一直不得民心，雖然屢次放盤，但數次都是價錢不合而未能成功。英國傳媒周三（15日）爆料，指Mike Ashley終於捨得放生「喜鵲」，將3億英鎊出售紐卡素，沙特阿拉伯王室的Public Investment Fund聯同英國商人Amanda Staveley及Reuben兄弟，極有機會成為新一代紐卡素班主。英媒得到轉手的31頁文件，並指出將需4周時間處理。

Mike Ashley（左）終於放生紐卡素。（Getty Images）

PIF的首腦正正是沙特王儲穆罕默德（Mohammad Bin Salman），34歲的他身家估計有70億英鎊，英媒指雖然Amanda Staveley為今次買方的領導人物，但事成後只會以3000萬英鎊得到10%股份，亦不會有任何實權，故可相信PIF才是紐卡素的真正新主人。

紐卡素賣盤，球迷最高興。（Getty Images）

DiBello與多名中東國家王室過從甚密。（Getty Images）

《華爾街日報》指出，美國名媛Kim Kardashian的朋友Carla DiBello，去年初向PIF建議收購紐卡素，更有可能是買方的談判人員之一。報道又指DiBello與穆罕默德「關係密切」，與PIF高層亦過從甚密，成為左右今次收購的重要人物。

惟《華》透露，PIF內部對開設顧問公司的DiBello甚為不滿，主因為「基金現今的主要項目發展不順」，卻在此時提出收購球會，目的只是從中獲利，但首腦人物對其主動「埋身」的動機及態度卻不以為然。
