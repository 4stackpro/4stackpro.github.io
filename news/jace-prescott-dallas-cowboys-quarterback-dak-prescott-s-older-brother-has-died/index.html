Jace Prescott, Dallas Cowboys Quarterback Dak Prescott's older brother, has died

(CNN) Jace Prescott, the older brother of Dallas Cowboys quarterback Dak Prescott, died Thursday, the team said in a statement.

“We are so saddened that Dak has lost his brother,” Jerry Jones, the team’s owner said in the statement.

“We want to help him get through this time. Whatever we can do to help him get through this tough time. When you’re young, and Dak is young, death is really hard to understand to think about or live with.”

Jace Pescott, 31, was the middle of three brothers – Dak and Tad. His cause of death has not been announced.

He played offensive line for four years at Northwestern State University in Natchitoches, Louisiana.

Dallas Cowboys draft pick CeeDee Lamb sends note to Dak Prescott

Shopping for another offensive weapon ahead of the 2020 season, the Dallas Cowboys and first-year coach Mike McCarthy landed a play-making gem on Thursday night in the first round of the NFL Draft, taking Oklahoma’s CeeDee Lamb at No. 17 overall. In a class loaded with elite-level wideouts, the Cowboys seized their opportunity at landing one of the Big 3 after Alabama’s Henry Ruggs and Jerry Jeudy had already come off the board.

Paired with Amari Cooper and Ezekiel Elliott, Lamb gives franchise quarterback Dak Prescott a third talented option next season. Lamb was asked after his selection what he thought about playing with one of the NFL’s top signal callers.

“He’s definitely going to get an ultimate competitor,” Lamb told ESPN after he got the call from Jerry Jones. “A guy that’s willing to learn and can’t wait to get used to helping him look like a better quarterback and he’s helping me look like a better receiver.”

A physically-gifted wideout, amb is coming off a noteworthy junior season featuring 62 receptions for 1,327 yards and 14 touchdowns on his way to All-American honors. He finished his Oklahoma career with 173 receptions for 3,292 yards and 32 touchdowns, individual marks that rank third, second and sixth in program history, respectively.

Dallas franchise-tagged Prescott last month, an effort to make sure he is the Cowboys’ quarterback this fall while the two sides work out a long-term extension. Prescott has reportedly not yet signed the franchise tender.

According to NFL.com insider Ian Rapoport, Prescott’s franchise tag number was recalculated and has now reached $31.4 million for the 2020 season. That’s an increase from $26.824 million, where it was when he was initially tagged on March 16. According to Rapoport, the tag price is based on the 2020 restricted free agent numbers and finalized after the restricted free agent period ends.

Prescott’s brother, Jace, died on Thursday unexpectedly. The 31-year-old was reportedly at home with his girlfriend, but no other information has been made available. The Cowboys confirmed the death and released a statement on Thursday night.

“It is with great sadness and support that we share the news of the passing of Jace Prescott today,” the statement read. “The loss of Tad and Dak’s brother is devastating. At this incredibly difficult time, the Prescott family asks only for prayers and respect for their privacy. "
