Bruno Labbadia turned down interest from abroad to come to Hertha

Bruno Labbadia turned down interest from abroad to come to Hertha By Laura Brandt

The new Hertha BSC coach, Bruno Labbadia, claims he turned down interest from abroad to come to Berlin.

Bruno Labbadia. Photo: RudolfSimon, CC BY-SA 3.0

Bruno Labbadia reveals he rejected the possibility of working abroad to become the new coach of Hertha BSC.

“Going abroad was also an option after being in the Bundesliga for so long. But Hertha were my preferred destination, in the summer as well. I see potential in the team and the club,” Labbadia said at his presentation on Monday.

The 54-year-old succeeds Alexander Nouri as coach with just nine Bundesliga games left to play this season.

Asked about the decision to take on the job now rather than in the summer, Lababadia responded:

“I never imagined taking on a new job this season. But the current season will have a big influence on the coming season. That’s why I can understand Hertha’s decision.”

Previously, Labbadia has coached Wolfsburg, Hamburger SV, VfB Stuttgart, Bayer Leverkusen, Greuther Fürth and Darmstadt.
