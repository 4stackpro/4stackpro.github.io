Over almost 20 years, Survivor has produced 38 unique winners that represent a wide variety of ages, stories and play styles. For the hit reality show’s landmark 40th season, 20 of those winners have come back to prove how they fare against those who share the title and show who is the best of the best.

Read on to find out everything we know so far about season 40, including when Survivor started, who is in the cast, who was voted out, when and where season 40 was filmed, a few spoilers and much more.

Who was voted out on Survivor Season 40?

Episode 1

In a jam-packed two-hour premiere, season 29 winner Natalie Anderson and season 8 winner Amber Mariano were voted off, due to their pre-existing relationships with other cast members.

Episode 2

Season 11 winner Danni Boatwright was voted off unanimously, after her paranoia caused her to try to flip on her alliance of old-school players.

Episode 3

Season 3 winner Ethan Zohn was the next old-schooler to go to the Edge of Extinction, as the majority wanted to weaken the rest of his group.

What’s the theme of Survivor Season 40?

In celebration of Survivor‘s 20th anniversary, season 40 will feature 20 previous winners who return to the game to see who is the best of the best. Though Survivor winners have returned in the past, this is the first cast that will consist of only winners.

Who are the 20 returning winners for Winners at War?

The cast of Survivor season 40 represents over 20 seasons, ranging from season 3 back in 2001 to last year’s season 37. They were officially revealed with release of the teaser trailer on Dec. 18, 2019. We’ll have many, many more details on each player in the coming weeks!

Adam Klein

Winning Season: Millennials vs. Gen X (Season 33)
Survivor means so much for Adam, considering it was a key part of his relationship with his late mother. Inspired by her, he played his heart out, finding idols, winning challenges, and taking risks, all of which earned friends and enemies alike. Reaching the final Tribal Council, he outlined his simultaneous loyalty and ability to get rid of threats. The jury then made his dreams come true, voting for him unanimously.

Amber Mariano

Winning Season: All-Stars (Season 8)
Coming from season 2, Amber found a partner both romantically and strategically in her now-husband Rob. The two of them ruled the roost from beginning to end, maneuvering over any obstacle in their way. Now, after a sixteen year Survivor hiatus, she’s back to see if that success can be replicated with a new group of returnees.

Ben Driebergen

Winning Season: Heroes v. Healers v. Hustlers (Season 35)
In the first half of his season Ben was a power player, a loyal ally who made tight relationships and was a key part of alliances both secret and open. But when everyone turned against him, the Marine went into fight mode. He found and played idols in three successive rounds, and became the first victor in the brand-new firemaking competition at the final four, all while putting on a show for the jury. They in turn gave Ben the win, honoring his fight.

Danni Boatwright

Winning Season: Guatemala (Season 11)
Danni impressively fought a numbers disadvantage the entire postmerge, losing all of her allies in the process. But her scrappiness and social maneuvering endeared her to those in power, eventually bringing her into the fold. The relationships she made throughout had a million-dollar value, as the bonds she made on both sides along with her underdog story earned her the win.

Denise Stapley

Winning Season: Philippines (Season 25)
Denise holds the distinction of going to every single Tribal Council in her season, only earning safety during one challenge. But she was able to impressively survive every time, building strong bonds with power players and quickly adapting her game across winnowing numbers and three different tribes. Now back again, the sex therapist hopes her second Survivor session yields fewer visits to Jeff Probst, but still the same result.

Ethan Zohn

Winning Season: Africa (Season 3)
Ethan played in a different environment compared to today, back when a tribe switch was considered the biggest twist in the show’s history. But his success speaks to a mentality that prevails: Despite Survivor‘s cutthroat nature, likability goes a long way. In the wilds of Kenya, Ethan’s natural charm and allegiances won out, making him one of the most popular winners of all time. And he’s back to continue the narrative, now with a personal story of survival.

Jeremy Collins

Winning Season: Cambodia – Second Chances (Season 31)
Jeremy was a constant in a season full of variables, aligning himself with the game’s biggest targets to avoid repeating history. As the game shifted, he kept his eyes on the prize: Winning for his wife and soon-to-be three children. With family on his mind, he made bold plays in the right moments, and used the concept of “voting blocs” to slip under the radar. As a result, Jeremy won unanimously, making good on his second chance.

Kim Spradlin-Wolfe

Winning Season: One World (Season 24)
The initial gender divide put Kim in a dominant alliance of five women, which she quickly became the leader of. Though the “One World” twist provided obstacles in her path, she worked around them to guarantee the girls would prevail while simultaneously keeping her options open. And, in one of the most impressive strategic games to date, Kim stood in an unloseable endgame, with everyone looking to take her to the finals out of loyalty and likability.

Michele Fitgerald

Winning Season: Kaôh Rōng (Season 32)
Never visiting Tribal Council in the premerge, Michele was raring to go once the merge hit, becoming a pivotal swing vote at two vital times. But her competition soon turned against her, fearing her connections. Through a series of events that include key challenge wins, a late-game medevac, and a juror removal, Michele claimed her spot at the end. And in one of the show’s most highly contested results, the jury honored her fight and impressive skill set.

Natalie Anderson

Winning Season: San Juan Del Sur – Blood vs. Water (Season 29)
The two-time Amazing Racer lost her support system from the get-go when her twin was voted out. She then bonded with future winner Jeremy Collins, who also got blindsided. Thus began Natalie’s revenge plot, timed to a tee. Part of the majority, she subtly swayed the numbers to turn votes against her allies, all the while mapping out her endgame. In a season run by pairs, it was the first player left single to take the victory, as Natalie was able to get her retribution and a million dollars to boot.

Nick Wilson

Winning Season: David vs. Goliath (Season 37)
Though in trouble early on, Nick ingrained himself into the cast by making several key two-person alliances, with nicknames to boot. He took advantage of the advantages the game provided, weaponizing them when outnumbered at the merge. And though he lost several key allies in the process, the public defender played to that “David” label, working his way out of several tight corners to ultimately prevail and join the winner’s circle.

Parvati Shallow

Winning Season: Micronesia – Fans vs. Favorites (Season 16)
After losing her first season, Survivor legend Parvati worked to bring more dimension to her game, though it was stymied when she was targeted early. Luckily, her quick connections prevailed, taking her from the bottom to a crucial role in several alliances. Part of some of the biggest moves in Survivor history, Parvati’s ruthlessness and charisma earned her the respect of the jury and fans alike.

Rob Mariano

Winning Season: Redemption Island (Season 22)
Rob made three runs at the game with moves out of The Godfather, but they never translated to a win until Redemption Island. Entering a cast of new players, Boston Rob played one of the most dominant games in history, assembling an army of fiercely loyal soldiers and easily winning in his handpicked endgame. Now, after writing the book on how to win Survivor, he’s back for a record fifth time to see if he could practice what he preaches.

Sandra Diaz-Twine

Winning Seasons: Pearl Islands (Season 7) and Heroes vs. Villains (Season 20)
Sandra is to this point the only person to win the title of Sole Survivor twice, claiming victory against some of the game’s biggest stars. She’s never afraid to mince words, creating rivalries with some of the most notorious villains reality TV has seen. But despite her outspoken personality, Sandra benefits from slipping under everyone’s expectations, willing to spin a lie or make a deal at a moment’s notice, as long as it’s not her.

Sarah Lacina

Winning Season: Game Changers (Season 34)
Sarah notably changed up tactics from her first time out, straying away from the safest route to become a silent assassin. In a hectic endgame, She was constantly in the know as to what was going on, influencing the outcome to satisfy her game and get the upper hand. Her style of masterminding, attention to detail, and social bonds got her to a win, showing she truly is a “game changer” in every sense of the phrase.

Sophie Clarke

Winning Season: South Pacific (Season 23)
Sophie was a key cog in her Day 1 alliance, working to make sure they got to the end without any deviations. Within that, she was part of a trio that ruled the game until Day 39. And sitting at the end, she used her position to her advantage, painting herself as the brains behind the operation while decrying her opponents’ flaws. It was ultimately a winning argument, as the jury diagnosed the then-medical student as the Sole Survivor.

Tony Vlachos

Winning Season: Cagayan (Season 28)
The police officer played hard from jump, lying up and down about his profession and unearthing an idol–one of three he would ultimately find. He was not shy from antics on the island, whether they be constructing a “spy shack” or trying to become proficient in llama. Though flashy, Tony played a game of influence, jumping sides frequently to get rid of friend and foe. But despite his betrayals, Tony’s ferocity, initiative, and ability to pivot led the jury to ultimately reward him.

Tyson Apostol

Winning Season: Blood vs. Water (Season 27)
Tyson suffered a series of setbacks early, with an eliminated girlfriend and injured shoulder. But he was able to not only make lemonade out of lemons, but chug that lemonade when nobody was looking. At the merge, Tyson took the reins and never looked back, cobbling an alliance of single players that he rode to the very end, where the jury awarded him for evading the target on his back.

Wendell Holland

Winning Season: Ghost Island (Season 36)
Wendell started the game by building furniture and a close connection with Domenick Abbate. The two would go on to dominate together, Domenick’s smarts and boisterousness complementing Wendell’s cool, level-headed thinking. The duo’s games were heavily linked, to the point where they tied the final vote, a Survivor first. But Wendell’s social navigating prevailed, as his bond with Laurel Johnson led her to break the tie in his favor.

Yul Kwon

Winning Season: Cook Islands (Season 13)
Yul’s rational and intelligent approach to the game made him the brains of his alliance, with an additional idol safety net. It also got him out of a tight spot, going from an 8-4 disadvantage to getting his alliance to the end, the biggest underdog story in the show’s history. With tenure at the White House, Facebook, and Google between seasons, we’ll see if Yul’s mind is still sharp for the twists and turns coming his way.

What are the twists for Season 40?

Winners at War will feature the return of the Edge of Extinction, a twist which debuted with the season of the same name the previous year. After a player is voted out, they will be given the option to either officially end their game or go to a remote island. Low on supplies and positivity, they would need to live there until a point in the season where they’ll compete in a challenge, where the winner will get to re-enter the game. In the eponymous Edge of Extinction season, Chris Underwood was voted out on Day 8 and stayed on the Edge of Extinction until Day 35, when he won the challenge and eventually the season.

What’s new for Season 40?

Season 40 will also introduce a brand-new game element in “Fire Tokens.” The tokens will serve as an in-game currency for people both in the game and on the Edge of Extinction. They can acquire Fire Tokens, then spend them on a list of things that range from comfort to advantages in the game.

What is the prize for the winner of Winners at War?

For the past 39 seasons, the winner of Survivor has won $1 million. However, this season the winnings have been upped to $2 million, making it the biggest single-person prize in reality TV history.

When and where was season 40 filmed?

Survivor: Winners at War was filmed near the end of May 2019 in the Mamanuca Islands in Fiji. The show has been filming in that location since its 33rd season in 2016.

How do I watch Survivor season 40?

Survivor airs Wednesday nights at 8 p.m. ET on CBS. Episodes will be made available the next day via the CBS All Access app.

Who won Winners at War?

At this time, the winner of Survivor: Winners at War is still unknown. Parade.com will update this article as soon as the Sole Survivor is crowned.
