Kate Middleton Thinks Prince Louis Looks Just Like Her

When Kensington Palace released pictures of Prince Louis for his second birthday Wednesday, it was easy to be distracted by the young royal’s playful spirit. His hands were covered in fingerpaint—arrayed in a rainbow, to honor NHS workers—and it seems amazing that photographer Kate Middleton was able to grab a picture in the first place. On closer look, it’s clear that Louis is growing into the spitting image of his mother, grin and all. According to People, Kate has also noticed the similarity.

A friend of Kate’s told the magazine that she has commented on it before. “Everyone always comments on how Louis is the spitting image of Kate,” the friend said. “She loves it and thinks it’s very sweet, she often jokes that he’s the only one of her kids who actually looks like her!”

The photos were taken in Norfolk, where Kate and Louis are self-isolating alongside dad Prince William and siblings Prince George, Princess Charlotte. The palace also used the opportunity to experiment with some memes. In a post on Thursday, they did their own twist on “Instagram vs. Reality,” adding an outtake that proved Louis was having a lot of fun with that fingerpaint:

The three Cambridge children really do look so similar that it’s practically uncanny. George and Charlotte, in particular, could have been mistaken for twins at age one, and that might be why it’s been so easy to see personality differences between them as they’ve grown up. (George is a little more reserved, and Charlotte is a firecracker.) They also looked just like their father at the same age. In the pictures of Louis released for his first birthday, he already was starting to look slightly more like his mother. Now, if you compare him to picture of young Kate, he looks that he might have her bubbly personality, too.
