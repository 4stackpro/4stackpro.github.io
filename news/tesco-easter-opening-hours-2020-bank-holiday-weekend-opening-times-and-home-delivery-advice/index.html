Tesco Easter opening hours 2020: Bank holiday weekend opening times, and home delivery advice

IT might be Easter Saturday today, but there’s still plenty of time to get an Easter shop in if you’re planning a Sunday roast.

Tesco is keeping its doors open on Saturday and Monday over the Easter weekend - here’s everything you need to know.

Here’s everything you need to know about Tesco opening hours over the Easter weekend Credit: AFP or licensors

Is Tesco open over Easter?

Opening hours at Tesco will depend on the type of store you’re visiting.

The supermarket has limited its general opening hours with stores closing at 10pm to help stop the spread of coronavirus.

On top of that, the Easter weekend means even more changes to opening times.

The majority of locations will be open except on the Sunday when stores in England and Wales will be shut.

Here’s the opening hours for the weekend:

Saturday - April 11

Tesco Extras and Metro stores will be open 6am - 10pm

Tesco Express stores will open for their usual hours.

Easter Sunday - April 12

Tesco, Tesco Extra Superstores and Metro stores will be closed in England, Wales and Northern Ireland

Tesco Extra Superstores and Metro stores will be open in Scotland from 8am - 8pm

All Express convenience stores will be open for their usual hours.

Easter Monday

Superstores and Metro stores in England and Wales will be open from 8am - 6pm.

Northern Ireland Tesco Extras will be open 10am - 7pm.

Scotland’s Tesco Extra Superstroes will be open 8am - 8pm.

All Express convenience stores are open usual hours.

While Tesco has confirmed that these are its typical opening hours, it says that some hours may vary by store, so you need to check the store locator.

Is Tesco doing home delivery?

Home delivery is operating as normal over the Easter break, but coronavirus means you might struggle to get a slot.

Each store has a limited number of delivery slots which are available on a first come, first serve basis. Because of recent increased customer demand, Tesco says its slots are being booked up faster than normal.

Lots of stores also offer Click+Collect slots which may be suitable for you if youâ€™re still looking to place an order.

You can also book a slot in advance and add a few items to your basket to secure it, which you can go back and update later.

At the time of writing there were no delivery slots available before April 27.

When are the Easter holidays 2019?

Good Friday falls on April 10 this year, while Easter Monday is on April 13.

Most schools have broken up for the holidays, but dates vary in different areas so you should check your school or council’s website.

It is not yet clear whether schools will open after the Easter break or if they will remain closed because of coronavirus.

When are the bank holiday dates for 2020?

Here is the full list of upcoming 2020 bank holidays in England and Wales.

As supermarket chain Asda reduces its opening hours at some stores due to the Coronavirus lockdown, it’s worth double checking opening times over the Easter break.

Despite some branches across the UK being open for shorter periods, Asda has added thousands more slots to its online delivery service after going on a hiring spree last month.

Now the chain has an additional 300,000 slots a week to its website asda.com - the equivalent of around 40,000 a day, making it easier for shoppers to get what they need, when they need it.

Stores across the UK are also operating priority hours for NHS workers every Monday, Wednesday and Friday from 8am to 9am.

Easter 2020 opening times

Saturday 11 April - All stores operating normal opening hours from 8am to 8pm.

  • All stores operating normal opening hours from 8am to 8pm. Easter Sunday 12 April - Asda stores in England and Wales will be closed. All stores in Scotland will operate normal opening hours.

  • Asda stores in England and Wales will be closed. All stores in Scotland will operate normal opening hours. Easter Monday 13 April - Most stores will open from 8am to 8pm.

Asda advises all customers to check opening times for their local store by using the online store locator tool.

To help its most vulnerable shoppers, Asda has also rolled out a new volunteer shopping card that allows customers to top up cards with credit.

The cards can then be used by helpers and volunteers to shop for others in store or online without the need to share bank details or cards.

It’s a helpful way for many who are self-isolating to get their shopping done in a fuss-free and manageable way.
