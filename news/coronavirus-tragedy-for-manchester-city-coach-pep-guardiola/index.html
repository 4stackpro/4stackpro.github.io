Coronavirus tragedy for Manchester City coach Pep Guardiola

Barcelona: Pep Guardiola, the Manchester City coach, has been hit by the devastating news that his mother Dolors has died after being diagnosed with coronavirus, the English Premier League champions said on Monday.

“The Manchester City family are devastated to report the death today of Pep’s mother Dolors Sala Carrio in Manresa, Barcelona after contracting coronavirus. She was 82-years-old,” City said in a post on their official Twitter account.

“Everyone associated with the club sends their most heartfelt sympathy at this most distressing time to Pep, his family and all their friends.” Guardiola, who has also coached Barcelona and Bayern Munich and spent most of his playing career with Barca, donated one million euros last month to fight against the virus, contributing to a campaign launched by the Angel Soler Daniel Foundation and Medical College of Barcelona.

Ian Holloway urges Pep Guardiola to get rid of Kyle Walker after sex party during coronavirus lockdown

Ian Holloway would consider getting rid of Kyle Walker if he was in Pep Guardiola’s shoes (Picture: Getty)

Ian Holloway says he would be looking to get rid of disgraced Manchester City star Kyle Walker if he was Pep Guardiola after the right-back broke coronavirus lockdown rules to host a sex party last Tuesday.

The Sun revealed on Sunday that Walker and his friend flouted the UK government’s strict measures by inviting two escorts to his flat. Just hours earlier, the 29-year-old had urged his Twitter followers to stay indoors amid the pandemic.

Walker has since apologised and is set to be fined two weeks’ wages by City. To add to this, the defender’s international future is in serious doubt, with England head coach Gareth Southgate said to be furious with his behaviour.

Man City boss Pep Guardiola’s mother dies after contracting coronavirus

Dolors Sala Carrio passed away aged 82 [photo/courtesy]

The mother of Manchester City manager Pep Guardiola has passed away after contracting coronavirus.

According to Manchester City, Dolors Sala Carrio passed away in Barcelona aged 82.

“The Manchester City family are devastated to report the death today of Pep’s mother Dolors Sala Carrio in Manresa, Barcelona after contracting Corona Virus. She was 82-years-old,’’ the club said in a statement on Twitter.

“Everyone associated with the club sends their most heartfelt sympathy at this most distressing time to Pep, his family and all their friends.”

Guardiola in March donated Sh120million (£920,000) to aid the fight against coronavirus in Spain.

“Pep Guardiola has made a donation of one million euros to the Fundacio Angel Soler Daniel for the acquisition and supply of health equipment to deal with the Covid-19 pandemic,” Angel Soler Daniel Foundation confirmed in a statement.
