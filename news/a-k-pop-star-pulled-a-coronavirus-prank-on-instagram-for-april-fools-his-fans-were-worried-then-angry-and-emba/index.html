A K-pop star pulled a coronavirus prank on Instagram for April Fools'. His fans were worried, then angry and embarrassed.

Korean pop singer Jaejoong of JYJ posted on Instagram that he had contracted COVID-19, later revealing that it was an April Fools’ prank intended to bring awareness to the implications of individual actions during the pandemic.

He later deleted the post and temporarily deactivated his account after enduring criticism from media and fans alike, with many finding the prank insensitive.

Later, he reactivated his account, posting a lengthy apology for his actions.

In what initially appeared to be a serious post, Korean pop star Jaejoong of boy group JYJ pulled a prank on his nearly 2 million Instagram followers by falsely stating that he had contracted COVID-19. Less than an hour later, per The New York Times, he backtracked and framed the prank as a move to raise awareness about the disease. Soon after, he deleted the post and temporarily deactivated his account.

“Although this did go quite far for April Fool’s Day, so many people worried about me in a short span of time,” he wrote in the revised Instagram post per Soompi’s translation. “I don’t think of this as an April Fool’s joke… It’s never!! just someone else’s problem. I wanted to tell you that protecting myself is protecting the precious people around us. I will accept all punishment I receive from this post. I hope all of you are healthy.”

Jaejoong had recently been promoting his most recent single in Japan and was scheduled to appear on NHK Radio’s “Furuya Masayuki’s PopA” show on April 1. Per Soompi, the show updated its website to state, “Jaejoong had been scheduled to appear, but due to personal circumstances there will be different content on the show today.” There’s currently no word from his company, C-Jes entertainment, but per Allkpop, it remarked that it was looking into the post.

Now, Jaejoong appears to have reactivated the account, posting a long apology.“The post today was too much, the method to get people to listen with his extreme attention hurt many people and received many criticisms,” he wrote, according to a fan translation from @shinkipeia on Twitter. “I would like to sincerely apologize for my post which have caused distress to the government agencies and medical staff who have been working hard for the coronavirus and many people who are working hard to overcome the lifestyle they have given up as they adhere to guidelines.”

Jaejoong, a 34-year-old idol who is a member of JYJ and a former member of boy group TVXQ, has faced criticism from media outlets around the world and also from his own fans for what many consider to be a tasteless joke. Many called for his cancellation by using the #JaejoongIsOverParty hashtag, dropping fancams and videos of their favorite idols while simultaneously denouncing the prank.

Other fans tweeted about how they felt embarrassed by his actions.

Many expressed anger about the decision, pointing out how ludicrous the action seemed and the potential impacts on Jaejoong’s career through memes.

There were still fans who defended Jaejoong’s actions, arguing that many didn’t understand that he was trying to bring awareness to the pandemic and the impact of individual actions, sacrificing his reputation in the process. Still, with a growing number of celebrities being diagnosed with COVID-19, the joke hit close to home for many fans. Despite Jaejoong’s apparent motivation to raise awareness about the implications of individual actions with the prank, many felt that he could have done so in a way that didn’t come across as taking the disease lightly.

It’s currently unclear whether Jaejoong will face legal or monetary repercussions for his actions. Per The Korea Herald, South Korean police said that “producing and peddling false information regarding the virus would be punishable under laws on obstruction of official duties and defamation.”

Kim Jaejoong, de JYJ, finge estar contagiado por coronavirus y levanta una gran polémica

Esta mañana saltaba la noticia de que Jaejoong, la mitad del dúo JYJ, estaba infectado de coronavirus. Así lo anunció él mismo en un post ya eliminado de su cuenta de Instagram, donde le siguen casi dos millones de personas. Lo que parecía un anuncio serio resultó ser una de las primeras bromas con muy poca gracia del April Fools, la fiesta de los inocentes que se celebra el primer día de abril.

Con un fondo negro y tono solemne, poco después del post original publicaba una rectificación, donde reconocía que su broma se le había ido de las manos y que su intención inicial era pedir a sus fans que tomaran precauciones para no contagiarse.

“Esto ha ido demasiado lejos para una broma del April Fools Day”, decía su explicación, también eliminada. “Mucha gente se ha preocupado por mí en un espacio pequeño de tiempo, y no pensé en ello como en una travesura. Tengo amigos y familiares enfermos, y algunos están muriendo”.

“Ya no es un problema de otros”, continuaba en su argumento. “Quería deciros que protegerse a uno mismo es como proteger las preciadas vidas de los demás. Además, aceptaré cualquier consecuencia por el post que publiqué, y espero que todos estéis sanos”.

Su peculiar manera de pedir a los fans que se protejan ya ha tenido consecuencias profesionales para el idol K-pop. Tiendas online han recibido cancelaciones de pedidos relacionados con JYJ, y su discográfica ha anunciado una investigación tras confirmar que el artista se encuentra de gira promocional por Japón.

Ayer mismo aparecía en el programa musical Utacon de la popular cadena NHK, junto a cantantes locales de J-pop. El mismo canal ha cancelado la aparición del coreano programada para hoy en un programa de radio, todo a causa de la polémica que ha protagonizado en las últimas horas.

Aunque algunos fans han salido por redes a defenderle, la respuesta mayoritaria en redes es que con estos temas no se juega. Está claro que para concienciar sobre las medidas y consecuencias de la enfermedad hay mejores formas que fingir que estás contagiado.
