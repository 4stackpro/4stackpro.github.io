Tory Lanez and Drake shatter Instagram Live records during ‘Quarantine Radio’

Thanks to nationwide “stay at home” mandates, services like Zoom and Instagram Live continue to gain popularity as they help those under quarantine stay connected. This week, Tory Lanez and Drake unexpectedly took it to the next level by shattering live stream records.

According to Hip Hop Wired, Tuesday, Lanez hosted an installment of his new venture called “Quarantine Radio.” He and French Montana were going back and forth in a hits battle.

Several other entertainers have had similar virtual battles but this one ended up separating the Canadian performer from the pack with heavy hitters. Justin Bieber, Lil Baby, Megan Thee Stallion, MTV, Roddy, Scott Storch, Amber Rose, Bryson Tiller, Casanova, and Chris Brown all joined in.

However, when Drake made his attendance known, engagement went into the stratosphere. At one time, it reached a whopping 315,000 viewers, an all-time record for Instagram.

Drake revealed that it was actually Brown who informed him of the gathering and convinced him to take part. Then he began answering questions while drinking shots.

“Before we take this shot, we have to shout out the fact that we’re the only two people in the game that have ever hit 300,000,” Lanez said.

The two Toronto natives also joked about Drake’s interaction with Kevin Durant and the rapper was asked if he tested positive for the coronavirus, which he denied once again. “Honestly, a glass of wine keeps corona away, boys,” Drizzy responded playfully.

Listen to Tory Lanez’ New Single ‘Who Needs Love’

Tory Lanez is doing the most.

In between hosting his record-setting “Quarantine Radio,” the prolific rapper-singer unleashes the new single from his upcoming mixtape The New Toronto 3. On “Who Needs Love,” Tory showcases his melodic side.

“Who needs love? / When I come around flexin’ / I don’t need no lovin’ from any one of my exes,” he sings. “I don’t need no stressin’, phone call, textin’ / Don’t kiss me, bitch, kiss my necklace.”

“Who Needs Love” follows “Do the Most” and “W” from The New Toronto 3, which is set to arrive April 10. The project, which follows last year’s Chixtape 5, will mark his final release on Interscope Records.

Amid the lockdown, Tory has been hosting “Quarantine Radio” on Instagram Live, with superstar guests including Justin Bieber, Chris Brown, DMX, Wiz Khalifa, and even Drake. His recent episode with Drizzy was watched by over 315,000 viewers.

G-Eazy, Tyga & Tory Lanez Share ‘Still Be Friends’ Music Video

G-Eazy, Tyga and Tory Lanez dropped off accompanying explicit and non-explicit music videos for their collaborative single, “Still Be Friends.”

The project is billed as a first-of-its-kind production collaboration with luxury adult content leader Vixen Media Group, orchestrated by Elie Maroun and G-Eazy. Directed by Daniel CZ, the visual exhibits a wild night out with the rapper and his friends, featuring appearances from several Vixen Angels and the likes of YG, Dre London, Mickey Avalon and Kossico.

“I was brainstorming concepts for the video and thought of the Playboy mansion scene from Once Upon a Time in Hollywood so I played it on mute and it miraculously synced with ‘Still Be Friends’, so we decided to run with a ‘70s theme. I think the ‘70s were an iconic time, especially when thinking about the adult entertainment industry. So, we brought on Vixen to provide the girls and their massive platform,” said G-Eazy. “The increased streaming numbers from adult sites with everyone having to stay inside became a no-brainer way to premiere the XXX version of the music video.”
