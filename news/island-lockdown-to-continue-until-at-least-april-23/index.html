Island Lockdown to continue until at least April 23

Rules on social distancing will be in place until at least April 23 and the Island’s borders will be closed for another month.

The Island’s Health and Social Care Minister David Ashford confirmed the news in afternoon’s Tynwald sitting.

He was speaking on behalf of the Chief Minister, who remains in self-isolation with mild symptoms of Coronavirus.

Mr Ashford said there will be no changes to the rules until at least Thursday, April 23 - and the Island’s borders will remain closed ’for at least another month’.

80-year-old man dies following Marine Drive crash

An elderly man has died following a serious crash near Port Soderick last night.

A major emergency response took place at around 7pm on Monday following reports a vehicle came off the edge of the road in the area between the Marine Drive junction and Port Soderick.

Police have now confirmed the driver - an 80-year-old man from Douglas who was airlifted to Noble’s Hospital following the incident - has now died as a result of serious injuries.

Officers say an investigation into the matter is ongoing and are asking people to avoid speculation and respect the privacy of the family at this time.

Authorities also want to hear from anyone who saw a blue Vauxhall Mokka car in the area of Port Soderick yesterday afternoon to establish more details - those who can help are asked to contact PC Hoy on Steffan.Hoy@iom.pnn.police.uk

Casualty airlifted to Hospital

A person has been airlifted to hospital after a vehicle went off the road at Marine Drive.

Douglas Coastguards were called out just before 7pm after a motorist and their vehicle which left the Marine Drive ended up to the south of Keristal beach.

Douglas Coastguards, paramedics, lifeboat, firefighters, police and a helicopter were all at the scene.

The casualty was recovered by the Coastguard Helicopter and transferred to Noble’sHospital for further treatment.
