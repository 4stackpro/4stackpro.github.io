The Script’s two shows for healthcare workers sell out in 30 minutes

Tickets to two HSE and NHS staff only concerts in Dublin with The Script sold out in half an hour.

The free gigs had been announced for the 3Arena in February next year.

The first concert announced was sold out within three minutes, while a second was added.

The band say they are overwhelmed by the response and say it’s the least they can do to thank frontline workers for all they’re doing to fight Covid-19.

“Myself, Mark and Glen are overwhelmed by the phenomenal response we‚Äôve had to the free shows for HSE and NHS (NI) staff and feel it’s the very least we can do to say an enormous and heartfelt thanks to their continued efforts in the fight against this virus,” said the band’s singer Danny O’Donoghue.

Ticket applications were only available to the eligible frontline staff who must bring their valid staff ID card - original format, no photocopies - to the show, a spokesperson for promoter MCD said.

The Script announce free Dublin concert for healthcare workers across Ireland

THE SCRIPT have announced plans for a free Dublin concert for healthcare workers across Ireland.

Frontline HSE and NHS workers along with Primary Care workers across Ireland will be eligible to receive tickets for the show, which will take place at Dublin’s 3Arena on February 9, 2021.

“We’ve always been huge supporters of those working on the frontline of our health care. They make sacrifices day in and day out, working harder than anyone. Not only to make sure we have access to resources in order for us to continue to live happy and healthy lives, but to also help us and our loved ones through some of the hardest points in our lives,” the band said.

“The very least for us to do in order to give back to their amazing sacrifices during this pandemic is getting to put on a free show for 13,000 HSE and NHS staff at the 3Arena in Dublin. We can’t thank them enough for their continued service to everyone, much more now than ever.”

Applications for tickets are limited to eligible frontline staff only with the option of a single guest and anyone applying must provide valid staff ID.

Ticket checks will be in operation.

All tickets issued will carry the name of the person making the original booking.

This name must match the one on the ID card submitted otherwise entry will be refused. Any guest attending alongside the eligible healthcare worker will need to be present and enter 3Arena at the same time.

Tickets will be limited to 2 per person - one eligible HSE/NHS, Primary Care and one guest - and will be made available online this coming Tuesday, April 7 at 7pm.
