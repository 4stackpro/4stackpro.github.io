Performing terawih prayers at home

KUALA LUMPUR: Mahadzir Nasaruddin had been leading the daily terawih prayers as the imam at Surau At-taqwa in Bukit Jalil – but not this year.

Mahadzir, 45, said this Ramadan would be different, as the fasting month usually means spending time with the community – the opposite of what social distancing measures demand during the movement control order (MCO) period.“Usually, there is a lot of communal activities during Ramadan, such as the nightly terawih prayers and enjoying a meal together after that.

“We also collect donations after the prayers to be given to the less fortunate.

“However, for this year, the surau committee will collect donations via social media to be donated to those in need, ” he said.

He understands that Muslims need to perform terawih prayers at home as safety comes first.

“One positive aspect of this is that we get to spend more time together with our families, ” he said.

The Muslim community performed their first terawih prayers last night, before the start of Ramadan today.

In George Town, retiree Mohd Yusoff Abu Bakar, 59, and his family used to perform the terawih prayers at a mosque near his house. But now, people are not allowed to go to places of worship due to the MCO.

He understands that personal sacrifices are needed to break the chain of Covid-19 infection.

“By staying at home, we can make sure that everyone is safe and healthy, ” he said when met at his flat in Batu Uban.

Masjid Al-Ittifaq chairman Abdul Jaafar Abdul Talib, 51, said his family would perform terawih prayers at home for the first time this year.

“I always pray at the mosque; I feel empty as I can’t go there for now. I understand that it is necessary for us to stay at home and reduce outdoor activities to break the chain of infection.

“I hope the pandemic can be contained soon so that our lives will be normal again, ” he said.

Sirajuddin Nordin, 49, who works in sales, supports the government’s measures to stop Covid-19.

“I was quite disappointed as we can’t do buka puasa (breaking of fast) with our extended family members and we can’t perform terawih prayers at the mosque.

“But it’s for the best, as I feel that all of us should stay at home rather than letting the disease spread to more people, ” he said.

In Johor Baru, infrastructure specialist Muhamed Bukhari Murad, 36, said that he usually performs terawih prayers at the mosque with his family during Ramadan.

“But we will perform the terawih prayers at home. It’s not a problem for me as the MCO is for the benefit of all of us, ” he said.

Businessman Muhammad Radzi Mohd Khaled, 39, said that he usually spends quality time with his parents and siblings during Ramadan.

“We will usually meet at our parents house in Bandar Baru Uda where we would all go to the mosque to perform the terawih prayers.

“However, as we are now in a new situation due to Covid-19, we will perform the terawih prayers at our own houses, ” he said.
