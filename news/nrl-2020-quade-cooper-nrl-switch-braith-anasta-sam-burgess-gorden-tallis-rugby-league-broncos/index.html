Braith Anasta believes NRL teams will terrorise Quade Cooper in defence if he is given a short-term deal for the rest of this season.

However Sam Burgess and Gorden Tallis would like to see Cooper have a crack at making the switch even though they conceded the defensive requirements would be immense.

“Quade is an exceptionally talented player and he has skills that most people dream of and it would be exciting to watch, but would it work?” Anasta questioned on Fox League Live.

“Quade reminds me a little bit of the style of Benji Marshall.

“I played rugby union until I was 16 or 17 and it is a lot different defensively in the halves.

“This late in his career to move into the halves position he would be terrorised, just terrorised by back-rowers.

“Having to make repeat tackles and get back up. Is he as effective in attack with those players running at his inside shoulder?”

Burgess also admitted that he would target Cooper in defence, but commended the former Wallaby for having the guts to put his hand up.

“Give him a shot, I would love to see him have a go,” Burgess said

“I would love to see it because he would get terrorised. If I was playing I would terrorise him and test him out. There is a bit of the cross code and I’ve done it.

“The transition is hard and there is not as many transferable skills as you’d imagine.

“The ball is the same shape, the field is the same, but the games are completely different.

“It’s chess and checkers and it would be hard for Quade to come and get a handle on the defensive system.

Quade Cooper and Tevita Pangai Jnr train at Red Hill. Source: News Corp Australia

“Angus Crichton came from rugby to Souths as a kid and it took him three or four years to get the grip of the defensive system.

“I’d love to see him give it a go and it is pretty brave of him to come out and say it so someone give him a shot.”

Tallis suggested fullback might be a better option for Cooper than the halves because of the defensive demands of halfbacks and five-eighths in modern rugby league.

“I wouldn’t terrorise him, but I would run at him a lot, but that is what you do these days,” Tallis said.

“I’d be so keen to watch Quade come into our game. I think he can attack brilliantly.

“When the Queensland Reds won the Super Rugby title Quade was a big part of that. He’s played on every big stage that rugby can offer.

“But rugby league is a totally different kettle of fish. I would love to see him come over and try his hand.

“I think fullback maybe to give him a little bit more space. In rugby league you attack off the front foot where as in union it is off the back foot, which would maybe help his game.

“But it is defence. You can’t hide, it is a jungle out there and they will find your weakness.”
