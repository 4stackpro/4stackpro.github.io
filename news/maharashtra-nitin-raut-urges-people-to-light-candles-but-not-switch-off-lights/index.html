NAGPUR: State energy minister Nitin Raut has appealed to citizens to light candles without switching off lights during Prime Minister Narendra Modi ’s nine minutes of black out call. Raut has released a press release and video stating that switching off lights all of a sudden may cause the grid to collapse.According to Raut if the grid collapses it will take minimum 16 hours for a generation plant to come back on line and it will take a week before normalcy returns to India. Other experts have said that India will be back on track within 12 hours with help of Nepal and Bangladesh.Raut has stated that due to the lockdown the demand in the state has reduced from 23,000 MW to 13,000 MW. “The state only has residential and essential services demand. The thermal power generation units have already been backed down and it is not possible to further reduce their output. Hence a sudden drop in demand will cause the grid frequency to shoot up and cause the system including the generation units to collapse,” he said in the video.Meanwhile, PowerGrid has asked all its officials to be on alert as there was a possibility of a grid collapse during the black out.A senior MSEDCL official said that hydroelectric generation can be switched off immediately and if load reduces suddenly, all hydro plants would be shut down. “We are hoping that there is no collapse,” he told TOI.
