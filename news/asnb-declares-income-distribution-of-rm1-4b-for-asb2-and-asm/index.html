ASNB declares income distribution of RM1.4b for ASB2 and ASM

KUALA LUMPUR: Amanah Saham Nasional Bhd (ASNB) declared income distribution per unit of 4.75 sen for Amanah Saham Bumiputera 2 (ASB 2) and 4.25 sen per unit for Amanah Saham Malaysia (ASM) totalling RM1.4bil.

ASNB, a unit of Permodalan Nasional Bhd, said on Monday the income distributions for the two fixed price funds were for the financial year ended March 31,2020.

The performance of ASB 2 and ASM is benchmarked against the Maybank 12-month fixed deposit.

“For FY ending March 31,2020, ASB 2 and ASM have exceeded the benchmark of Maybank 12-month FD, where the annual total returns for the funds were recorded at 4.75% and 4.25% distribution rate respectively, compared to the 2.60% return recorded by Maybank 12-Month FD, ” it said.

Elaborating on the distribution, it said ASNB declared an income distribution per unit of 4.75 sen to 439,543 unit holders for ASB 2 and 4.25 sen to 553,188 unit holders for ASM.

As at Feb 29, ASB 2 recorded a net realised income of RM413.30mil whilst ASM recorded a net realised income of RM646.70mil.

ASNB also declared income distribution for three variable price funds amounting to RM228.7mil.

The first was ASN Equity 3 where the income distribution per unit was 3.25 sen to 89,327 unit holders.

The second was for ASN Imbang 2 at 3.50 sen per unit to 86,426 unit holders while for the third, ASN Sara 1, it was 4.55 sen for 69,872 unit holder.

The total returns of the three funds are benchmarked against the FBM 100 and Maybank 12-Month FD, at a ratio customised to each fund.

“Despite a challenging year in 2019, PNB’s funds’ portfolios have held up in relative terms, outperforming their respective benchmarks largely due to the exposure in high-quality stocks with strong fundamentals and the steps taken to diversify the portfolio further, ” it said.

It pointed out the computation of income distribution for ASB 2 and ASM was based on the average minimum monthly balance held throughout the financial year of each respective fund.

While for ASN Equity 3, ASN Imbang 2 and ASN Sara 1, it was based on the end-year balance held.

The distribution declared by these funds will be re-invested as additional units into the accounts of unit holders and will be automatically credited into unit holders’ accounts on April 1,2020.

On the outlook, PNB said due to the current unprecedented conditions, investors will continue to see companies facing earnings pressure and this will not only be confined to the Malaysian market, but globally as well.

“While the economic impact from current events will be widely felt, as an investor with a longer-term horizon, the expectation is for the market to recover over the medium and longer run, after establishing a new normal.

“In adapting to these changes, PNB is focused on tapping into the prospects offered by the market mispricing to ensure that it will be able to ride through this volatility.

“PNB will continue to build the resilience of its investment portfolio amidst this highly volatile market backdrop as its investments undergo a rigorous evaluation process, and as the investment recommendations go through a multi-layer governance process.

“This is to ensure PNB’s portfolio comprises well-managed companies that can ride through the market cycles, ” it said.

ASNB to close branches from 4pm on Friday

KUALA LUMPUR: Amanah Saham Nasional Bhd (ASNB) will close its branches nationwide from 4pm on Friday and will remain closed until March 31.

Permodalan Nasional Bhd said on Thursday despite the closure of the branches, unit holders can continue to perform transactions at ASNB’s network of appointed agents comprising key banks and Pos Malaysia, or online via the myASNB portal or myASNB mobile application.

“Transactions related to estate claims and Hibah Amanah can only be processed at ASNB branches and as such, will be temporarily unavailable until March 31,2020, ” it said in a statement.

PNB said it sought the public’s understanding on this matter and would provide updates in due course.

“We call on our unit holders and all Malaysians to strictly abide by the restrictions stipulated under the Movement Control Order and stay at home as much as possible throughout this period, ” it said.

PNB is one of the largest fund management companies in Malaysia with assets under management (AUM) exceeding RM300bil.

As for ASNB, it currently manages 14 unit trust funds to reach almost 254 billion units in circulation (UIC) and over 14 million accounts.
