Rihanna Rihannas Her Way Out of Another Album Update

The world’s president, Robyn Rihanna Fenty. Photo: Robin L Marshall/Getty Images for BET

Okay, fine, we can’t even be mad at Rihanna right now. The singer has provided yet another non-update on R9 while talking to British Vogue, adding to a long list of swerves. But last week alone, Rihanna donated $700,000 worth of ventilators to Barbados and $5 million to various organizations fighting the coronavirus pandemic, so we get that she’s kind of busy being a head of state. Normally, Rihanna says she’s “very aggressively working on music.” Note the vague use of “music,” not R9. For all we know, Rihanna is talking about her recent feature on PartyNextDoor’s new song “Believe It,” which is definitively not the reggae album she’s been teasing. As for that, the only thing Rihanna has to say is that she’s doing things her way, as if that wasn’t obvious from her Instagram clap backs. “I can’t say when I’m going to drop … There are no rules,” she said. “There’s no format. There’s just good music, and if I feel it, I’m putting it out.” The musician-entrepreneur is enjoying her freedom, bouncing from Fenty Maison to Fenty Beauty to Savage X and back to music. “I feel like I have no boundaries,” she added. “I’ve done everything — I’ve done all the hits, I’ve tried every genre — now I’m just, I’m wide open. I can make anything that I want.” Just, please, for the love of God, release it!

Rihanna Was Shocked by Fan Reactions to Fenty Beauty

Rihanna has shown us all that there is nothing she can’t do. The popstar burst on the scene 15 years ago as a protégé of Jay-Z, and from there, she has made a name for herself. From countless hits and albums to her humanitarian efforts, the Bajan songstress is multifaceted and iconic.

In addition to her musical talent, Rihanna has also dove into the fashion industry with her fashion house, FENTY and lingerie line, Savage x Fenty. However, it was her beauty brand, Fenty Beauty that truly began to solidify her status as a mogul.

When Rhianna launched Fenty Beauty in September 2017, people ran online and to Sephora to get their hands on the inclusive brand. For years, despite the numerous labels in the beauty industry, Black and brown women struggled to find makeup that would fit their skin tones and textures. Now, Rihanna has revealed why she was so shocked by fan reactions to Fenty Beauty.

Rihanna created Fenty Beauty for everyone

For years, Rihanna perfected her go-to makeup technique, refining it and testing out different things before launching Fenty Beauty. Now, the line includes foundations, highlighters, lip glosses, lipsticks, mascaras, shadows and everything in between.

Rihanna had one singular mission in mind when she launched her brand. “Fenty Beauty was created for everyone: For women of all shades, personalities, attitudes, cultures, and races,” Rihanna said in a statement. “I wanted everyone to feel included. That’s the real reason I made this line.”

According to Forbes, after just 15-months of business Fenty Beauty generated an estimated $570 million in revenue.

Rihanna writes all of the copy for Fenty Beauty

The fashion and beauty mogul is super hands-on with her Fenty Beauty brand. While the foundations are numbered, Rihanna is solely responsible for coming up with the copy for the entire line of products.

“I write all of the copy for the websites, the product descriptions, product names, the color names…” she told British Vogue. “I do have a huge team, but I just don’t necessarily think their tone is mine. I’d feel like a fraud selling something that I can’t stand by.”

This is why Fenty Beauty has such bomb product names like the Stunna Boss Nude lipsticks or the “Who Needs Clothes?!: Body Lava Body Luminizer” Honestly, who wouldn’t want to buy that? Still, despite the mega-success of her makeup brand, Rihanna was absolutely shocked by fan reactions when she launched it.

Rihanna was disappointed by Fenty Beauty being called groundbreaking

With over 40 shades of foundation alone, Fenty Beauty has set the tone for inclusivity in the beauty industry, forcing other brands to scramble to catch up. Still, Rihanna was not prepared for this reaction.

“I’m shocked by people saying, ‘Oh my god, what made you think of making make-up for black girls?'” she explained to British Vogue. “I’m like, ‘What? You thought this was like, a marketing strategy? Like I’m a genius?’ It’s shocking most of the time. Then it turns into disappointment that this is groundbreaking right now. In my mind, this was just normal.”

Rihanna finally gave us a new song, sort of

(CNN) It’s not the new album the world has been begging for, but it’s a start.

Early Friday, Canadian singer PartyNextDoor dropped his new album, “PartyMobile,” which features Rihanna on the song, “Believe It.”

Rihanna announced the new single on her verified Twitter account.

Folks have been more than excited for new music from the singer and makeup mogul since she dropped her last album, “Anti,” in 2016.

“Best make me believe it / Best make me believe it / Believe you won’t deceive me,” she and PartyNextDoor sing on the chorus.
