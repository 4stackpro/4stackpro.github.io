Hedgehog award for Fitzwilliam College

Fitzwilliam College has achieved the Hedgehog Friendly Campus Bronze Award thanks to staff, students and Fellows rescuing and rehoming hedgehogs.

The volunteer group has been working hard to make sure that the College grounds are a haven for hedgehogs.

The Hedgehog Friendly Campus Scheme is run by the British Hedgehog Preservation Society, and encourages teams of staff and students to participate annually. University teams sign up, then work towards accreditation by completing a number of ‘hedgehog-friendly’ actions.

Alex Nikolin, who has been heading up the campaign on behalf of the JCR, said: “We were so thrilled to be Oxbridge’s first Hedgehog Friendly Campus. We’ve seen two hedgehogs that we rehomed from Shepreth Wildlife Conservation Charity (SWCC) around and about.

“It’s great to know that we are playing our part in helping this vulnerable species and raise awareness of the difficulties facing hedgehogs due to habitat loss.”

Fitzwilliam College first got involved last year with the national accreditation scheme with a 15-strong team who want to make the College a safer place for wild hedgehogs.

In conjunction with SWCC, Fitzwilliam College has been using its grounds and gardens as a release site for hedgehogs that have spent time at the charity’s animal hospital.

Steve Kidger, Head Gardener at Fitzwilliam College, said: “Over the years we have established hedgehog friendly habitat, hog hotels and feeding stations in the College grounds.

“In addition, by eliminating of pesticide usage and introducing log piles and hedgehog highways, we should help to boost the resident population.”

To get involved contact info@hedgehogfriendlycampus.co.uk and follow the campaign on Facebook and Twitter.
