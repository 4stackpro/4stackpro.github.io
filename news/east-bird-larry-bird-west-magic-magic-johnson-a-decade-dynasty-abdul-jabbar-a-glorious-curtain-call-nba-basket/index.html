東鳥(Larry Bird)西魔(Magic Johnson)，十年王朝：傳奇賈霸

Kareem Abdul-Jabbar與湖人總教練Pat Riley合作近十年，但他從來不是Riley管理方式的粉絲，Magic Johnson說表面上兩人相處看起來沒什麼問題，但身為隊友他能夠感覺到橫亙在這兩位傳奇之間的緊張感，Pat Riley對待Jabbar的方式與湖人其他球員並不相同，而Jabbar也不只一次宣稱Riley那套對湖人諸將頗具效用的激勵方法，對他來講一點用處都沒有。Jabbar認為那只是Riley操縱球員的方式罷了，Jabbar不喜歡”被激勵”，因為他自己便是最能激勵自己的人，何時要打球，何時不要，這件事是Jabbar自己說了算！

籃球從來就不能完全佔據Jabbar的生活，籃球不是一切，對Jabbar來說打籃球只是他賴以維生的工作，他有眾多的興趣，好比說文學、音樂、政治、藝術、宗教或種族議題…等，他從來就不把球賽帶回家，而在1980年代初－中期，他依舊是想宰制全場就宰制全場，每當有一個新的中鋒踏進NBA，Jabbar就會用他自己的、一種安靜的方式讓對方知道誰才是老大？

但到了1989年，夕陽餘暉，他的宰制能力就跟他的頭髮一樣逐漸凋零，這看在Magic眼裡當然有點難過，因為Big Fella擁有這麼漫長的職業生涯，在山巔上維持了這麼久的歲月，他是個驕傲的人，而現在他必須要帶著那股傲氣，逐步走下NBA舞台了。如果Jabbar的衰退對湖人小老弟造成了影響，那麼自不待言，對天勾本人的影響更是巨大，當他離開球場，也是一個時代的終結，畢竟當時在聯盟執牛耳的，是其它位置的巨星好比說Magic Johnson、Larry Bird以及Michael Jordan。但Jabbar就像是一頭老獅子，他需要更長的休息時間，但是獅子畢竟是獅子，如果你在他需要平靜的時候不斷地去打擾他，那麼他也會不吝將他的”威猛”示人！

直到最後一季，Kareem Abdul-Jabbar依然都是湖人隊的重要拼圖，自從1984年Elvin Hayes退休起，Jabbar就已經是整個聯盟裡年紀最大的球員了，但沒有Jabbar的貢獻，湖人是不可能拿下這麼多座冠軍的。在一個競爭激烈，大多數球員只打幾年就會離開的聯盟中，很難想像在場上屹立二十年歲月，或者是好比說連續787場比賽得分上雙，這到底是什麼樣的一種概念呢？

1984年春天，Jabbar逼進了由Wilt Chamberlain所創下的生涯總得分紀錄(31,419分)，最後Jabbar是在一場對猶他爵士隊的比賽中打破紀錄的，Magic Johnson賽前已經告訴所有的隊友，Jabbar破紀錄的那球一定要由Magic手上傳出！而比賽中每當Jabbar投進一球，現場的氣氛就愈緊張熱烈，最後就剩一顆球的差距了，球隊叫出暫停，Pat Riley特地為Jabbar設計了一個戰術，暫停後Magic在外圍運球，他密切觀察著Jabbar在禁區要位的狀況，等到Jabbar卡到了一個他自覺舒適的位置，Magic馬上把球傳進去，Jabbar拿到球後做了幾個假動作，接著啟動他那最強的攻擊武器－天勾，從十五呎外勾進了那破紀錄的一球！當他命中的時候，全場都瘋狂了，裁判叫停，把那顆籃球送給了Jabbar，湖人的隊友相當興奮，賽後他們還一起去開趴，不過呢，Magic說他不知道Jabbar是怎麼慶祝的，因為他沒出席，可能是留在自己旅館房間吧？

1989年4月23日，這是該年湖人最後一場例行賽，也是Kareem Abdul-Jabbar生涯最後一場例行賽，Jabbar要在論壇球場向球迷告別。他的父母到場參加，他的兒子Amir唱了國歌，而當湖人眾將被介紹出場時，他們全隊都帶著Jabbar招牌的護目鏡，比賽還剩兩分鐘時，Jabbar投進了他例行賽生涯最後一球(當然是由Magic傳給他的)！當天Jabbar收到了許多退休禮物，其中最隆重的是一台純白色的勞斯萊斯，那是Pat Riley的想法，資金則是來自湖人全體隊員與教練團的貢獻。送勞斯萊斯(還直接開到球場上)這種事在NBA是聞所未聞的，但就如同Magic Johnson說的，Jabbar的存在同樣也是籃球史上聞所未聞的！在典禮中，Magic Johnson代表湖人全隊發言，他看著勞斯萊斯，又看著Jabbar，深情款款地說：你看這台車，它當然不便宜，但我們所有人知道，這些年來你為了球隊貢獻了什麼。這麼多年下來，你把我們揹在你的背上前進，所以現在我們決定要送你一樣東西，它可以在你退休後，帶著你前進……

Johnson & Johnson partners with BARDA to fund $1 billion in COVID-19 vaccine research – TechCrunch

Pharmaceutical giant Johnson & Johnson is partnering with the Biomedical Advanced Research and Development Authority of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services to fund over $1 billion in COVID-19 vaccine and antiviral treatment research and development, the company said on Monday.

The partnership is an expansion of an existing agreement between BARDA and J&J’s Janssen Pharmaceutical Companies division.

With the agreement, the company is setting a goal of providing a global supply of more than one billion doses of the vaccine, which J&J expects to have in clinical trials by September 2020 at the latest. The first batches of the vaccine may be available for emergency use by early 2021, the company said.

BARDA’s partnership with J&J encompasses research and development of potential antiviral treatments in addition to the work that’s being done to develop a vaccine for the disease. Those efforts include development work J&J and BARDA are conducting with the Rega Institute for Medical Research in Belgium.

J&J said it had also committed to expanding its global manufacturing capacity, both in the U.S. and overseas. That additional production ability will help the company bring an affordable vaccine to the public on a not-for-profit basis for emergency pandemic use, the company said.

Working with teams at Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center, a part of the Harvard Medical School, Janssen Pharmaceuticals began its research into potential vaccine candidates back in January. Those candidates were tested at several academic institutions, the company said, which led to the identification of a lead COVID-19 vaccine candidate — and two potential back-ups.

Last week, Moderna Health, another pharmaceutical company working on a vaccine, said that it could have an experimental treatment available to healthcare workers as soon as the fall.

The Moderna vaccine uses messenger RNA, rather than doses of the COVID-19 virus itself, to inoculate against the disease. The use of mRNA means that the inoculation doesn’t expose recipients to the disease itself, so they’re not at risk of contracting the disease.

Last Monday, Moderna made the vaccine available to volunteer participants as part of the company’s Phase 1 clinical trials conducted in Washington state.

Opinion | Boris Johnson Needs to Learn From Andrew Cuomo

LONDON — Now that Prime Minister Boris Johnson has become the first head of government to test positive for coronavirus, he will have to change the way he operates as he seeks to lead his country through the crisis. Other leaders — it is inevitable he will not be the last to have to self-isolate and “work from home” — will be interested to see how he adapts to what will be a very strange working environment for someone used to a steady flow of meetings, able to summon advisers into his presence at will.

However, it is also a good opportunity to take stock of his crisis management style, including the way he communicates to the public. Mr. Johnson has shown in his rise that he can be a very effective communicator. But the style — deliberately bumbling and disheveled; fond of jokes; disregarding facts and details in favor of bluster — does not lend itself to a crisis as grave as this.

As someone who has known Mr. Johnson for several decades, and never hidden my view that he is not fit to be prime minister, I do not imagine he will listen to any advice I give.

But I am nonetheless offering it, that he should use his isolation to develop a new way of communicating: more fact, more detail; less rhetoric, less bluster; cut the homilies and rambles; fewer snappy one-liners; more empathy for the dead and dying, and those caring for them; more explanation of decision making; more linking of new policy announcements to previous ones, and to data; use of graphics and film to explain; and, please, comb your hair! This is not a trivial point. In times of crisis, people look to leaders for confidence and strength. If you look disorganized, people fear that you are disorganized.
