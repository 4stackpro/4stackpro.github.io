Alex Rodriguez, Jennifer Lopez advocate staying home after controversial gym visit

Sign up for our special edition newsletter to get a daily update on the coronavirus pandemic.

Alex Rodriguez and Jennifer Lopez are sending a new message in regards to the global coronavirus pandemic.

Less than a week after the power couple was criticized after going to their local gym in Miami, despite Florida Gov. Ron DeSantis’ statewide stay-at-home order, A-Rod and J.Lo released a video on Twitter Wednesday pleading for people to stay at home to help prevent the spread of the coronavirus — after New York Gov. Andrew Cuomo tagged the two on Twitter asking, “who are you staying home for?

“We want you to please stay at home to stop the spread of COVID-19,” Lopez said. “We’re staying home to protect those on the front lines, the doctors, the nurses, and technicians that are working so hard to save lives at our hospitals.”

Rodriguez, 44, and Lopez, 50, were granted special access to their local gym last week despite signs on the building reading, “This gym is not open. Stay home, stay safe.”

Security with the couple were seen wearing protective masks and opening the car door with what appeared to be a sanitizing wipe.

As of Tuesday, Florida had more than 14,700 confirmed cases of the coronavirus, according to Johns Hopkins.
