Driver caught speeding at 110mph on M1 after he ‘had been to buy bread’ in London

Police have fined a man who was caught speeding back to Nottingham at 110mph on the M1 and told them he had been to London to buy bread.

The man was stopped by officers at about Sunday April 5 travelling to Nottingham northbound on the M1.

They said he had been in the car with his two young children and claimed bread in London was £1 cheaper.

A Leicestershire Police spokesperson said: “At around 10.40pm on Sunday April 5, an officer from the road policing unit witnessed a vehicle travelling in excess of the speed limit on the northbound carriageway of the M1.

“The vehicle was stopped shortly after junction 22. The driver was issued with a fixed penalty notice under the Road Traffic Regulations Act and a further fixed penalty notice under the Health Protection Regulations 2020.”

The distance between Nottingham and London is more than 120 miles.

A fixed penalty notice was given by the officers who stopped the driver but given the speed they were reportedly driving at, the driver will automatically be reported to court.

The second notice was under the Health Protection Regulations 2020, which comes with a £60 fine. It came into force last month to give police powers to punish anyone flouting lockdown restrictions.
