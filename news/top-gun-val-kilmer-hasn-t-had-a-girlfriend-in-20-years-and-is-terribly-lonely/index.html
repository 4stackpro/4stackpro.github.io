Top Gun’s Val Kilmer hasn’t had a girlfriend in 20 years and is terribly lonely

Val, pictured last year, rose to fame in the 80s (Picture: ZUMA Wire/REX)

Val Kilmer opens up about his relationship status in his upcoming autobiography, it has been reported.

The movie icon says he has been single for the last 20 years, and experiences loneliness every day.

You have to admire his honesty.

Val is the star of films like Top Gun and Batman Forever, and an extract of his 2020 autobiography I’m Your Huckleberry has been shared by People.

‘I haven’t had a girlfriend in 20 years. The truth is I’m lonely a part of every day,’ the 60-year-old writes.

Val is a parent to two kids named Mercedes, 28, and Jack, 24, with ex-wife Joanne Whalley.

Former couple Val and Cher pictured in 1990 (Picture: The LIFE Picture Collection via Getty Images)

Val’s famous exes include actress Angelina Jolie, supermodel Cindy Crawford and Believe singer Cher.

Speaking about his love for women, Val – who’s appearing in this year’s Top Gun movie – added: ‘I’ve always found women infinitely more interesting than men.

Another pic of Cher and Val, because they were an amazing couple! (Picture: WireImage)

‘Perhaps that’s why we’ve always gotten along.

‘We [men] are big oafy elephants… and they are butterflies.’

Val also starred in the original 1989 movie Top Gun with Tom Cruise (Picture: Paramount/Kobal/REX)

Val also addresses his 2015 battle with throat cancer in the book, crediting Cher with supporting him through his darkest days.

‘For her true friends, her steadfast love and loyalty never die.’

Cher is praised by ex Val Kilmer for supporting him through his cancer battle

Val Kilmer has praised ex Cher for supporting him through his cancer battle

Cher’s ex Val Kilmer has been praising her for sticking by him through his cancer battle.

The Top Gun star speaks highly of former flame Cher in his new memoir, I’m Your Huckleberry, and reveals how she helped him get through his diagnosis.

The two previously dated in the 80s and Val, 60, was later diagnosed with throat cancer in 2015.

Cher invited him to stay with her during his illness and he recalls how Cher ‘stepped in and stepped up’ to be there for him.

In an excerpt from his new book, published by People, Val recalls one night when Cher had been on hand, after he woke up vomiting blood.

He writes: ‘One night I suddenly awoke vomiting blood that covered the bed like a scene out of The Godfather.

Cher and Val dated back in the 80s and he stayed with her following his cancer diagnosis in 2015 (Picture: Getty)

‘I prayed immediately, then called 911. Then alerted my hostess. Cher stepped in and stepped up. And yet even in my grave condition, I saw her scanning the paramedic, who was Gregory Peck drop-dead handsome. Only in Hollywood, right?

‘Despite the fact that I was covered in blood, I caught her eye and bounced my brows like Groucho Marx. Hubba hubba. Cher was bashful to be busted but then couldn’t help laughing out loud at the audacity.’

Val continues: ‘Here we were, joking about beauty and desire, while I looked like a stunt man from Tarantino’s Reservoir Dogs and, yes, while my life seemed to be in mortal danger.

‘We laughed out loud before they finished with my vitals and shut me up with an oxygen mask.’

Val reveals in his memoir that he is ‘grateful’ to be cancer free for four years now, after fearing he could lose his voice and undergoing a tracheotomy, chemotherapy and radiation.

He is set to reprise his role as Admiral Tom Iceman Kazansky in the new Top Gun movie, Top Gun: Maverick, which is due to hit the big screen in July later this year in the UK.

So far, the movie hasn’t been delayed due to the coronavirus pandemic, despite many other new releases, including new James Bond movie No Time To Die, being postponed to much later in the year.

Kilmer and Tom Cruise are both back for new Top Gun movie Top Gun: Maverick (Picture: Paramount/Kobal/REX)

Tom Cruise is also returning in Top Gun: Maverick and will be back as Pete Maverick Mitchell, who he teases will be doing things that have ‘never been done’ this time round.

‘We just started talking and I realised that there were things that we could accomplish cinematically,’ he told Empire.

Why is Val Kilmer Relevant Again?

As with any actor that makes it big, there are usually a couple of roles along the way that really define a career. For Kilmer, there were a couple of roles that really allowed him to reach the top of the Hollywood elite at the time.

Back in 1986, he was part of one of the most famous movies of all time in Top Gun. In which he played Tom “Iceman” Kazansky. He then went on to play Jim Morrison in a biopic titled “The Doors” in 1991. In 1995 he became Bruce Wayne/Batman for Batman Forever. From there though things kind of came downhill.
