Pink breaks down as she details terrifying coronavirus ordeal

Pink was left praying for her son after she and three-year-old Jameson both contracted coronavirus.

The singer spoke to Ellen DeGeneres about her ordeal with Covid-19, revealing her son was the first to start showing symptoms.

She explained: ‘It started with Jameson, actually, and you know, he’s three — three-year-olds get sick all the time.’

The family went into quarantine on 11 March, and Jameson’s symptoms soon started to worsen, developing a fever, severe chest pains and diarrhea.

Pink managed to get a test for herself, which came back positive though she never had a fever, but suffered with a sore throat and worrying breathing difficulties.

She has asthma, but has not needed hospital treatment or a nebuliser in 30 years, but she found her inhalers weren’t helping much as she struggled to breathe.

Pink and her son, Jameson, caught it but her daughter Willow and partner Cary Hart did not (Picture: BACKGRID)

Despite her own symptoms, Pink admitted she was more worried for her son, choking up as she told Ellen: ‘At one point I was praying, crying, praying – I realise how ridiculous I sounded – like I thought they told us our kids were going to be ok.

‘We were told that our kids were going to be ok, and I think when people started explaining what this disease is, it was too early to be able to name it completely and tell everybody what to look out for.’

Pink wanted to warn people that the virus doesn’t just affect the elderly or those with existing conditions, adding: ‘I got it the second-worst [out of my family]; I’m 40, I have asthma and I’m fit as a fiddle.

The singer and her son are feeling better after their ordeal (Picture: FilmMagic)

‘It’s not just affecting people over 65.’

She also addressed the anger that she had been able to get her hands on a test, admitting: ‘I would say, you should be angry that I can get a test and you can’t.

‘But being angry at me is not going to help anything, it’s not gonna solve the issue of the fact that you can’t get your hands on a test.

‘You should be angry about that and we should work together to try and change that.

‘And number two, tell me anybody with a sick three-year-old that if they could get their hands on a test wouldn’t take it. And if they say that, I’m calling bulls**t.”
