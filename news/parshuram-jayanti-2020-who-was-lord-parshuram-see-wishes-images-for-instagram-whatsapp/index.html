Lord Parashurama is also called the sixth avatar of Shrihari Vishnu. In the scriptures, Lord Parashurama is also called the ‘God of the Crow’.

Lord Parashurama was a brahmin with Kshatriya like qualities. He used to take any action in the anger. Due to his anger, once he had made earth devoid of Kshatriya for twenty-one times.

What is the importance of Akshaya Tritiya?

Akshaya Tritiya is said to be the main festival of Sanatan Dharmis. On this day, the results of all the deeds such as bathing, donation, etc. come back to you. The fast is known as Akshay. In order to get success in the desired field, one should give donations to the needy and poor in terms of food, etc.

When is Parashuram Jayanti celebrated?

Parashuram Jayanti is celebrated on Tritiya tithi of Vaishakh Shukla Paksha. Tritiya tithi starts from 11:00 AM on April 25 to 11:00 AM on April 26, 2020. It is believed that Lord Parshuram was born on Tritiya tithi of Shukla Paksha of Vaishakh month from the womb of wife Renuka.

What people do on Parshuram Jayanti?

According to popular belief, people tend to buy gold and silver jewelry on Parshuram Jayanti. The trend to buy gold and silver has increased over years. It is said buying gold, doing good works on this day gives you good returns.

How should be Parashuram Jayanti celebrated this year?

This time due to the Covid-19 lockdown, it is advised to celebrate the festival at homes safely.

Parshuram Jayanti 2020: Wishes, images and quotes to observe birth anniversary of Lord Vishnu’s sixth incarnation

Parshuram Jayanti 2020: Bhagwan Parshuram is said to be the sixth incarnation of the Hindu god Vishnu and is considered as the anniversary of the birth of Lord Vishnu. The day is marked as a significant festival of Parshuram Jayanti in the country. This day is also known as Akshaya Tritiya and is considered a good day to start or do anything important.

It is the third day of the month of Vaishakh, Trikiya of Shukla Paksha. This year, Parshuram Jayanti 2020 will be held on April 25.

Due to coronavirus lockdown, people won’t be able to visit the temple this year, however, they will continue their Parshuram Jayanti 2020 celebrations at their home, observing fasts and reciting hymns and devotional songs.
