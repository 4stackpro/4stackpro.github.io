Benzema defends his dig at Giroud: 'I told the truth'

Real Madrid striker Karim Benzema has stated he “simply told the truth” regarding his jibe at French compatriot Olivier Giroud.

Real Madrid striker Benzema said “don’t confuse Formula One with karting” when asked on Instagram live about comparisons to Chelsea centre-forward Giroud.

Giroud has filled the void left by Benzema’s continued international exile, which dates back to 2015, helping France to World Cup glory in 2018.

Benzema sought to clarify his comments on Monday, stating that he had also talked up Giroud’s strengths and adding that the same comparison could be used between himself and ex-Brazil superstar Ronaldo.

However, Benzema doubled down with his assessment.

Real Madrid’s Benzema on Giroud jibe

Speaking on Instagram, he said: “About Giroud, I simply told the truth.

“But we didn’t remember what I said about him, especially about what he brings to the French national team.

“We just remembered the moment when I said I was F1 and he was karting. That’s what I think, that’s the truth, and that’s how it is.

“Because now, if you ask me with R9 (Ronaldo): R9 is F1, and I am karting. That’s the way it is. We’re going to move on.”

Benzema scored 27 goals in 81 international appearances for France, while Giroud is third on the all-time list with 39 from 97 matches.

Prior to coronavirus postponing the likes of LaLiga, the Premier League and Euro 2020, Benzema had scored 19 goals across all competitions for Zinedine Zidane’s Madrid in 2019-20.

Benzema: Real Madrid’s thermometer

Karim Benzema’s season has largely mirrored Real Madrid’s form in the 2019/20 campaign.

The striker has been the best thermometer in evaluating Los Blancos’ health throughout the season, as shown when the Frenchman’s goals dried up in 2020, which had a knock-on effect on his teammates.

The reverse theory is also valid, though, as one could argue that the team ran out of gas, lost freshness in games and had less offensive power, with Benzema being a victim of his own loneliness up top.

He’s a goalscorer

With the exception of the last month and a half, Benzema was the guide of a team that had received lots of praise.

He had been fighting for the Pichichi Trophy with Lionel Messi until the coronavirus-enforced stoppage of play, despite his drought in the last stretch.

Benzema has 19 goals, well above the rest of a team whose second-top scorer is Sergio Ramos, while the Frenchman is five goals behind the top scorer in Spain: Messi.

The 32-year-old has 14 strikes in LaLiga Santander, but Real Madrid’s dependency on his inspiration is a fact.

Ramos is the second-best ‘striker’ with seven goals, five of which have come in LaLiga Santander, and this clearly says a lot about the lack of contribution from Benzema’s teammates.

Benzema has not found a reliable partner up top. Eden Hazard played in a pair with the Frenchman, but the Belgian’s injuries have prevented them from striking up a fearsome relationship on the pitch.

The absence of Cristiano Ronaldo

This solitude that Benzema has been feeling must be extended to the last two years, which coincides with Cristiano Ronaldo’s departure.

Since the Portuguese superstar left for Juventus, Ramos has been Real Madrid’s second-top scorer with 18 goals.

All of Europe’s big names have a pair in attack that make the difference: Cristiano and Paulo Dybala at Juventus; Sergio Aguero and Raheem Sterling at Manchester City; Kylian Mbappe and Neymar at Paris Saint-Germain; and Messi and Luis Suarez at Barcelona.

Benzema has scored 35 goals since Cristiano left, an average of 0.54 per game, which can be compared to Luis Suarez’s 0.64, Antoine Griezmann’s 0.41 and Alvaro Morata’s 0.37. Then there is Messi, who scores almost one goal per game (0.98).

We have to add Benzema’s records, too: 10 years at Real Madrid as a forward; a vital part of the best Real Madrid since the team that won five European Cups; the sixth-top scorer in the club’s history; and the third-top scorer in Champions League history.

Bad start in 2020

The turn of the year was not favourable for Benzema, who has netted just two goals in 2020.

Once football gets back underway, whenever that is, the Frenchman will be looking to make up for lost time goalscoring-wise.
