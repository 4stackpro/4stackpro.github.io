Saturday Kitchen Live has been forced to explain itself as furious viewers moaned the show was not properly social distancing its guests.

The cooking show, which by its nature usually airs live on Saturday morning, today decided to run a pre-recorded episode featuring Girls Aloud star Kimberley Walsh, which was filmed before coronavirus lockdown guidance came into effect.

Host Matt Tebbutt told the camera: “Good morning. Now before you settle down to watch this episode of Saturday Kitchen, we need you to know we recorded this show as an Easter treat before the lockdown, before government guidelines on how to stay safe.

“Saturday Kitchen is back live next Saturday and I’ll be here live on BBC1 every weekday from 10am starting this Easter Monday with a brand new show, Daily Kitchen Live.

“Jack Monroe will join me to help give you the most up-to-date advice on what to cook now to stay healthy and make the best possible tasty dishes. I hope you enjoy this show, and happy Easter.”

But despite Matt’s warning, some sleepy-headed viewers were a little slow to catch on.

“Was this show pre recorded or live?? Social distancing is clearly not in place here…..” tweeted one at the show’s official account.

“Just thinking this has bern filmed before the social distancing measures kicked in?” said another.

A third demanded to know: “Is this actually live? Or recorded pre social distancing???”

“Has #SaturdayKitchen given up on social distancing? Or is this a repeat?” wrote another.

And a fifth commented: “Whoever made this decision to broadcast this is an idiot.”

One joker replied: “Absolutely, was watching Indiana Jones last night, defying the stay at home advice. Fuming.”

Other viewers cottoned on straightaway, but felt the show had proved how much has changed in the last few weeks.

“Tuned into @SaturdayKitchen a few minutes late & thought it had all just been a terrible dream… Then I realised it was a pre-record from back in the glory days when we were allowed to be near each other. What a miserable realisation,” one pointed out.

A weary Matt kept repeating throughout the show that there would be no live vote as the episode had been pre-recorded, meaning special guest Kimberley’s food heaven or food hell would be “decided by fate”.

Matt will return to live cooking from Easter Monday, when he and Jack Monroe launch their very first Daily Kitchen show, which seeks to help those in lockdown discover new meals and kitchen tips.

The programme will air weekdays from 10-11am.
