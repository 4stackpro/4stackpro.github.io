Sebastian, Florida Coronavirus Update: 44 Indian River County Cases

Health officials are reporting 3 confirmed coronavirus cases in Sebastian, Florida.

As we stated yesterday in our Sebastian Daily newscast, we expected the numbers to go up.

There are 44 cases in Indian River County, Florida. 6 patients are non-residents. Brevard County, our neighbor to the north, now has 51 confirmed COVID-19 cases.

The Indian River County patients are 26 men and 18 women between the ages of 8 to 84. There are 6 people hospitalized, and no deaths.

About 436 people tested negative in Indian River County.

Cleveland Clinic, Indian River Hospital, is providing drive-through testing by appointments from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m., seven days a week. To make an appointment, call the hospital at 772-226-4846.

Florida

The big concern right now is the state of Florida. The daily cases are soaring steadily.

Florida now has 9,585 total cases (9,248 are residents, 337 are non-Florida residents), and there have been 163 deaths. Over 1,215 people are hospitalized in the state. About 82,137 people have tested negative in Florida.

United States

According to numbers by the CDC, there are 245,646 cases, and there have been 6,068 deaths.

The number of people recovering from the virus is 9,311 in the United States (almost doubled since yesterday).

Global

Around the world, there are 1,039,166 cases of coronavirus and over 55,092 people have died. Approximately 219,019 people have recovered from the virus worldwide.

Sebastian

Right now there are still 3 coronavirus cases in Sebastian. We expect this number to go up. Please follow the CDC guidelines and stay home.

For now, if you are 65 or older, or have an illness or chronic condition, please stay home.

If you need need groceries or other items delivered to your home, call Blue River Delivery at 772-643-6880.

Sebastian man arrested on warrant, state files charges in 2019 fatal crash

CLOSE If you see a crime being committed, leave anonymous tips with Treasure Coast Crime Stoppers. GINNY BEAGAN/TCPALM April 2019 Wochit

INDIAN RIVER COUNTY — A Sebastian man was arrested on a March 26 warrant charging him in a 2019 crash that killed his 25-year-old passenger on Roseland Road.

Dylan Owens (Photo: CONTRIBUTED PHOTO BY INDIAN RIVER COUNTY SHERIFF’S OFFICE)

Dylan Owens, 25, of the 500 block of Gerald Street, was charged with vehicular homicide and DUI manslaughter in the Aug. 14, 2019 death of Corey Weber.

Florida Highway Patrol reports stated Owens and Weber were traveling in the 1200 block of Roseland Road when the 2012 Dodge vehicle driven by Owens left the road and struck a tree around 3:30 a.m.

A state prosecutor signed the warrant for his arrest March 26, according to court records.

He was released on $25,000 bail just before 2 a.m. April 1.

At his first appearance in court Thursday a judge ordered that he was prohibited from drinking alcohol and would be under house arrest with GPS monitoring until his trial.

His arraignment is scheduled for May 1.
