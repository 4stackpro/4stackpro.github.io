Jaylon Johnson goes No. 50 to Bears in 2020 NFL Draft

The Chicago Bears have decided to add a little bit of talent to the defensive backline in the 2020 NFL Draft. With the No. 50 overall pick, the Bears have selected Jaylon Johnson, a cornerback out of Utah. Johnson left the Utes after his junior season to pursue NFL dreams.

A four-star prospect out of Fresno, California, initially, Johnson got on the field early in his career for the Utes. He played 12 games, starting two of them, as a true freshman in 2017 and tied for the team lead in pass breakups with six. The next year he was First Team All-Pac-12 after starting all 14 games for the Utes at cornerback.

His junior year, 2019, Johnson really impressed the country. He started 13 games for Utah but skipped the Alamo Bowl against Texas after declaring early for the NFL Draft. He recorded 36 tackles and had a team-high 11 pass breakups. Johnson was named a Second Team All-American by multiple outlets and was First Team All-Pac-12 for the second-straight season.

Steve Bartle, the publisher of UteZone.com on the 247Sports network, believes that his patience and eye-recognition are two strong points for Johnson in the NFL.

At the 2019 NFL Draft, Johnson record a 40-yard dash time of 4.5 seconds, did 15 reps on the 225-pound bench press and had a vertical jump of 36.5 inches. His performance gave him a grade of 6.43, which, according to NFL.com, means he will be a starter within the first two years in the league.

NFL.com analyst Lance Zierlein drew comparisons to Shaquill Griffin. Griffin was a third-round pick out of UCF in the 2017 NFL Draft by the Seattle Seahawks, where he still is today. In three seasons with the Seahawks, Griffin has 186 tackles and three interceptions.
