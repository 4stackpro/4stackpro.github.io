How to say Happy Easter in French, Spanish, Italian and other languages

Easter is being celebrated across the world (Picture: Getty Images)

It’s Easter Sunday, and although this year’s celebrations might be somewhat different in the wake of the coronavirus pandemic, people across the world are still marking the day with greetings, online services and of course lots of chocolate.

And one way to send your Easter greetings to someone elsewhere in the world could be to wish them a happy Easter in their native language.

So just how do you wish someone in another part of the globe a happy Easter?

Here’s what you need to know…

How to say Happy Easter in French

If you want to send someone Easter greetings in French, you can say ‘Joyeuses Paques’.

How to say Happy Easter in German

Just because you’re in lockdown doesn’t mean you can’t enjoy Easter eggs (Picture: Martin Schutt/Getty)

If you’re sending Easter greetings to a German-speaking native, the correct term is ‘Frohe Ostern’.

How to say Happy Easter in Welsh

If you want to wish greetings of the season to friends in Wales, you can say to them ‘Pasg Hapus’.

How to say Happy Easter in Italian

If you’re sending Easter wishes to Italian-speaking friends, you can greet them with ‘buona Pasqua’.

Don’t forget about the hot cross buns

How to say Happy Easter in Spanish

If your Easter greetings are for people in Spain, you can tell them ‘Felices Pascuas’.

How to say Happy Easter in Dutch

If you’re delivering an Easter message to a Dutch speaker, you can say ‘gelukkig Pasen’.

How to say Happy Easter in Portuguese

Egg hunt are likely to be happening in gardens across the country (Picture: Getty Images)

For Portuguese speaking friends or family, the correct term is ‘Feliz Pascoa’.

How to say Happy Easter in Polish

If you’re wishing a Polish person Happy Easter, you can do so by saying ‘Wesolych Swiat Wielkanocynch’ – or use the Easter greeting ‘Wesolego Alleluia’.

How to say Happy Easter in Greek

If you’re sending a greeting to Greek speakers, you can wish them ‘Kalo Pascha’.

How to say Happy Easter in Gaelic

If you want to greet Gaelic-speaking Irish friends you can do so by telling them ‘Casca sona’ – or, if it’s Scottish Gaelic they’re speaking you can say ‘Caisge Shona’

Local hardware firm donates 2,000 Easter eggs to Ireland’s frontline healthcare workers

A LOUTH hardware store has donated 2,000 Easter Eggs to hardworking frontline staff at two major hospitals in Ireland.

Dundalk-based firm Toolfix delivered the chocolate to brave workers at Navan General Hospital and Our Lady of Lourdes Hospital in Drogheda this past Easter Sunday.

A small but welcome gesture, the Ecco Road-based company provided the sweet treats as a thank you for the incredible work and dedication shown by those treating Ireland’s sick and vulnerable during this coronavirus pandemic.

While in years gone by the Easter Weekend has provided a welcome break for many, this year healthcare workers have found themselves busier than ever and facing off against arguably their biggest challenge yet.

It’s a situation the good people over at Toolfix are only too aware of.
