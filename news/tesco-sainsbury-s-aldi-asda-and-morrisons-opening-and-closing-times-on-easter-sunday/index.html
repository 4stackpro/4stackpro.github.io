The ongoing coronavirus has already seen supermarkets stripped bare of certain items, as well as being forced to introduce restrictions on certain products.

Opening times have also been disrupted - and furthe changes are likely with Easter Sunday now only hours away.

Here is a list of major supermarkets across Devon and Cornwall - and when they will be open.

Tesco

Tesco has limited its general opening hours with stores closing at 10pm to help stop the spread of coronavirus.

Tesco, Tesco Extra Superstores and Metro stores will be closed in England, Wales and Northern Ireland, but Express convenience stores will be open for their usual hours.

Sainsbury’s

NHS staff will have priority Monday to Saturday from 7.30am to 8am, while elderly customers, carers and disabled customers have a dedicated shopping hour from 8am to 9am Monday, Wednesday and Friday.

These dedicated shopping times will be in place over the weekend.

Most Sainsbury’s stores will be shut on Easter Sunday - but not all.

Their store locator can tell you when each individual store will be open.

Asda

The majority of Asda stores will be open on Saturday and Monday between 8am and 8pm. However, on Easter Sunday, all of their stores will be closed.

Waitrose

All stores in England and Wales will be closed.

Morrisons

Easter Sunday will see all Morrisons stores closed.

Aldi

All stores in England and Wales will be closed. All stores in Scotland will open until 6pm.

Co-Op

Opening times will vary from store to store on a regional basis, so check on the Co-Op website for more details.

Is Tesco open over Easter?

Opening hours at Tesco will depend on the type of store you’re visiting.

The supermarket has limited its general opening hours with stores closing at 10pm to help stop the spread of coronavirus.

On top of that, the Easter weekend means even more changes to opening times.

The majority of locations will be open except on the Sunday when stores in England and Wales will be shut.

Here’s the opening hours for Easter Sunday and Monday:

Easter Sunday - April 12

Tesco, Tesco Extra Superstores and Metro stores will be closed in England, Wales and Northern Ireland

Tesco Extra Superstores and Metro stores will be open in Scotland from 8am - 8pm

All Express convenience stores will be open for their usual hours.

Easter Monday

Superstores and Metro stores in England and Wales will be open from 8am - 6pm.

Northern Ireland Tesco Extras will be open 10am - 7pm.

Scotland’s Tesco Extra Superstroes will be open 8am - 8pm.

All Express convenience stores are open usual hours.

While Tesco has confirmed that these are its typical opening hours, it says that some hours may vary by store, so you need to check the store locator.

Is Tesco doing home delivery?

Home delivery is operating as normal over the Easter break, but coronavirus means you might struggle to get a slot.

Each store has a limited number of delivery slots which are available on a first come, first serve basis. Because of recent increased customer demand, Tesco says its slots are being booked up faster than normal.

Lots of stores also offer Click+Collect slots which may be suitable for you if you’re still looking to place an order.

You can also book a slot in advance and add a few items to your basket to secure it, which you can go back and update later.

At the time of writing there were no delivery slots available before April 27.

When are the Easter holidays 2019?

Good Friday fell on April 10 this year, while Easter Monday is on April 13.

Most schools have broken up for the holidays, but dates vary in different areas so you should check your school or council’s website.

It is not yet clear whether schools will open after the Easter break or if they will remain closed because of coronavirus.

When are the bank holiday dates for 2020?

Here is the full list of upcoming 2020 bank holidays in England and Wales.

Friday, April 10 (Good Friday)

(Good Friday) Monday, April 13 (Easter Monday)

(Easter Monday) Monday, May 8 (Early May Bank Holiday)

(Early May Bank Holiday) Monday, May 25 (Spring Bank Holiday)

(Spring Bank Holiday) Monday, August 31 (Summer Bank Holiday)

(Summer Bank Holiday) Friday, December 25 (Christmas Day)

(Christmas Day) Monday, December 28 (substitute Boxing Day)

Dates in Scotland and Northern Ireland vary.
