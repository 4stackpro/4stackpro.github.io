Is Easter Monday a public holiday or a bank holiday?

Easter isn’t quite over (Picture: Martin Schutt/Getty)

It’s Easter, which means for most of us, a nice long four-day weekend, which began with Good Friday and ends with Easter Monday today.

While the coronavirus lockdown may have put paid to a lot of the usual Easter celebrations, people across the world have still been making the most of the break – from embracing the religious aspect through to scoffing chocolate eggs in front of some seasonal films.

Today’s the final day of the Easter weekend – but is Easter Monday a bank holiday or a public holiday?

Here’s what you need to know…

Is Easter Monday a public holiday or a bank holiday?

Yes today, Easter Monday, is a bank holiday – although make sure you check with your employer that bank holidays are part of your statutory leave before you take the day off.

Once Easter Monday is over, England, Wales and Scotland have five more bank holidays in 2020, while Northern Ireland has six.

How many bank holidays are left in 2020 and when is the next?

If you still have Easter chocolate left now’s the time to finish it (Picture: Getty Images)

The next bank holiday is in early May, on Friday 8 – moving from a Monday to a Friday for this year only to mark the 75th anniversary of VE Day.

However, it remains to be seen whether the coronavirus lockdown in the UK will have been lifted by then.

The remaining bank holidays in the UK are as follows:

Spring bank holiday: Monday 25 May

Battle of the Boyne (Northern Ireland, substitute day): Monday 13 July

Summer bank holiday (Scotland): Monday 3 August

Summer bank holiday (England, Wales and Northern Ireland only): Monday 31 August

Christmas Day: Friday 25 December

Boxing Day (substitute day): Monday 28 December

If you’re running low on Easter treats today, checking supermarket opening times before leaving the house is a good idea.

Whether you need ingredients for dinner, reduced chocolate Easter eggs or some freshly baked hot cross buns, Morrisons is well stocked with all the seasonal goodies for the bank holiday.

However, with changing opening times due to the Coronavirus lockdown, shops are set to open and close at slightly different hours to usual today.

The retailer advises shoppers to check online using the my.morrisons.com/storefinder for details of their local branches.

Morrisons Easter opening times

  • Normal opening hours 8am to 8pm(NHS hours from 7am to 8am for all frontline staff with an NHS badge Easter Sunday 12 April - All Morrisons stores in England and Wales will be closed. All stores in Scotland will operate normal opening hours.

Stores across the UK now also have a dedicated hour of shopping time for all NHS staff from Monday-Saturday between 7am and 8am, as well as 30 minutes to browse from 9am-9.30am on Sundays.

If you’re planning to shop online, Morrisons advise you to do so way in advance as unprecedented demand has led to lack of availability of delivery slots.

There are also restrictions on key essentials to prevent stockpiling.
