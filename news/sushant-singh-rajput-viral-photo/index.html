Sushant Singh Rajput'‘s last pic with Rhea Chakraborty goes viral

Mumbai, June 14 (IANS) Soon after the news of actor Sushant Singh Rajputs sudden death sent shockwaves all over, an old picture of the actor with his rumoured girlfriend Rhea Chakraborty has gone viral on social media.

The photograph, in which they were spotted outside their gym in Mumbai, is said to be their last picture together. They were snapped together on March 11.

In the picture, Sushant is seen wearing a blue printed T-shirt and black shorts, while Rhea is seen in a galaxy print yoga shorts and a grey top.

Sushant and Rhea used to often workout together. They were snapped outside their gym several times.

In an interview to IANS, earlier this year, Rhea said she prefers no intrusion into her private life, and that is the reason she never reacts to the rumours of her relationship with Sushant.

Asked if all the recent headlines about their reported affair takes away her focus from work, Rhea had told IANS: “Sushant is a dear friend. I have known him for many years! No more comments on this.”

On Sunday, Sushant was found hanging at his Bandra residence here, by his domestic help who alerted the police.

The actor hailed from Bihar and was educated in Patna and New Delhi, before shifting to Mumbai.

After establishing himself on the small screen with “Pavitra Rishta”, he transitioned to the big screen with film “Kai Po Chhe”. He went on to do projects including “Shuddh Desi Romance”, the biopic “M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story”, “Kedarnath” and “Chhichore”, among several others.

“Pics Shared, Family Harassed; Sushant’s Death Reminds Us How Insensitive We’ve All Become”

2020 has truly been a year of terrible losses for the Indian film industry as we lost some of its brightest gems. The news of TV actor turned Bollywood star Sushant Singh Rajput’s death by suicide has shocked the entire nation and has left us all shattered.

Image credit: Instagram, Sushant Singh Rajput

Sushant’s death has left his friends, family, and fans completely devastated. It’s understandable that the circumstances of his demise makes us, as his followers, want to know more about it but how we’re dealing with it is extremely insensitive!

Image credit: Instagram, Sushant Singh Rajput

Insensitive media coverage

When a celebrity suffers a terrible fate, it’s not entirely unethical to report about it to the world but how it’s done makes a lot of difference. If you have a moderate social media presence, you’ll know that while speculating the reasons for Sushant’s death, people have been discussing his relationships, his bank balance, and his struggle with depression.

A disturbing trend where some are sharing pictures of Sushant’s body has also surfaced. Is this how we want to pay our respect to the deceased? How insensitive have we become as people to the plight of others? We should stop sensationalising grief and respect the privacy of his loved ones who are already struggling with his sudden death.

Lack of privacy

Earlier last month when veteran actor Rishi Kapoor died, we saw many people sharing photos of him and his family members at the funeral. Some even trolled Ranbir and Alia’s behaviour and attire. Sushant’s family in Patna has been hounded by reporters asking for comments. Calls were made to his former girlfriend Ankita Lokhande asking about the same.

Just because the deceased was a celebrity, it does not give us, as his audience, the right to invade the privacy of people close to him. We have to be more empathetic to the grieving and not be so quick in making judgements about his life or treat his death as gossip. It is also high time we started normalising conversations around mental health and depression.

Image credit: Instagram, Sushant Singh Rajput

Remembering Sushant

For someone who was struggling to cope with his mental health, the best way to remember him is to not dissect his personality or inspect the reasons behind his death. It’s high time we reevaluated insensitivity and called out people as well as media outlets that treat news like this so irresponsibly.
