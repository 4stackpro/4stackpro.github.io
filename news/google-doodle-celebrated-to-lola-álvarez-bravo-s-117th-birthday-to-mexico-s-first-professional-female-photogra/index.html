Google Doodle Celebrated to Lola Álvarez Bravo’s 117th Birthday to Mexico’s first professional female photographer – Broadcast Cover

Today’s Doodle commends one of Mexico’s first expert female picture takers, Lola Álvarez Bravo, on her 117th birthday celebration. Known for her representations of open figures, just as road photography chronicling many years of Mexican life, she is viewed as one of the nation’s pioneers of innovator photography.

Conceived Dolores Martinez de Anda in Jalisco, Mexico, on this day in 1903, the future picture taker moved to Mexico City as a kid. It was from her neighbor, Manuel Bravo, that she previously took in the essentials of photography, incorporating creating photographs in the darkroom. The pair wedded in 1925, and both proceeded to accomplish colossal recognition for their work.

Álvarez Bravo turned into a focal figure in Mexico’s post-transformation social renaissance, and among her most universally famous photos were those taken in the mid-1940s of her companion, and one of the nation’s most notable craftsmen, the painter Frida Kahlo. Through her photojournalistic focal point, Álvarez Bravo caught scenes of regular Mexican life, from nearby customs to outside barbershops, depicting the profundity and expansiveness of the nation’s way of life over a vocation crossing the greater part a century.

In 1981, Álvarez Bravo’s home province of Jalisco granted her an award of qualification for her commitment to expressions of the human experience, and after four years, a plaque was introduced in her respect in Guadalajara’s notable Degollada Theater.

¡Feliz cumpleaños, Lola Álvarez Bravo! Much obliged to you for catching Mexico from the beginning.

Lola Álvarez Bravo, la primera fotógrafa mexicana, es homenajeada por Google Actualidad El gigante de Internet dedicó su Doodle a la artista que trabajó a lado de Frida Kahlo

Su trabajo no pasa desapercibido. Lola Álvarez Bravo, la primera fotógrafa mexicana, recibió un Doodle como homenaje de parte de Google a su trayectoria.

Fue una reportera gráfica, fotógrafa comercial, documental, retratista y artista gráfica. Sus obras son consideradas como una ‘biografía visual’ del México del Siglo XX.

Manuel Álvarez Bravo fue quien la introdujo al mundo de la fotografía. Su exesposo le enseño cómo revelar películas, así como, hacer copias en el cuarto oscuro.

Lola Álvarez Bravo fue influenciada por grandes de la fotografía universal como lo fueron Edward Weston y Tina Modotti. A su vez, colaboró con figuras mexicanas como Diego Rivera, Rufino Tamayo, David Alfaro Siqueiros y Frida Kahlo.

Lola Álvarez Bravo, the portraitist of everyday modernism

Although he was not clear about his vocation, Lola Álvarez Bravo she knew from a young age that she would not work in anything, something that she would later remember through the following thought from her childhood: “As a child, with my father, I thought that I had to do something that was not common and ordinary, even though I was educated very poorly, They educated me for everything useless, because what I hated the most in my life is that they ordered and limited my freedom. ”
