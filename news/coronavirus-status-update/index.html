Coronavirus status update

Due to the coronavirus (COVID-19) outbreak many of the national floorball leagues have been effected and also the upcoming IFF Events and multi-sport events are continuously being evaluated. Since the last update on March 20th, new decisions have been made in Austria, Belgium, France, Latvia, Netherlands, Poland, Singapore, Slovakia, Spain and Slovenia.

Almost all national leagues have been cancelled for this season and in most countries no champions are crowned. In Poland and Sweden, the regular season winners, were crowned national champions 2020:

Champions in Poland: Interplastic Olimpia Osowa Gdańsk women & KS Górale Nowy Targ men

Champions in Sweden: Iksu women & Falun men

Below you can find an update on the situation based on the information that the IFF has been able to find out from its members. Please note that the situation might change quickly (and that the IFF might not have all the latest information).

Austria

All floorball events and leagues are cancelled for this season. The 3 nations (AUT, HUN, SLO) floorball league Final Four is postponed to May 1-17 (final decision will be taken in mid-April).

Belgium

All floorball events and leagues are cancelled for this season.

Canada

All matches in the Floorball League of Canada have been suspended until further notice.

Czech Republic

All floorball events and leagues are cancelled for this season.

Denmark

All floorball events and leagues are cancelled for this season.

Estonia

All floorball events and leagues are cancelled for at least two months (decision made on March 11th).

Finland

All floorball events and leagues are cancelled for this season.

France

All floorball events and leagues cancelled for this season.

Germany

All floorball events and leagues have been cancelled for this season.

Italy

All floorball events are cancelled until at least April 3rd (new decision to be made soon).

Latvia

All floorball events and leagues are cancelled for this season.

Netherlands

All floorball events have been cancelled for this season.

New Zealand

All floorball related activities are suspended at least until 14th of April.

Norway

All floorball events and leagues are cancelled for this season.

Poland

All floorball events and leagues have been cancelled for this season. Regular season winners, Interplastic Olimpia Osowa Gdańsk women & KS Górale Nowy Targ men, were crowned champions.

Singapore

All floorball events and leagues have been cancelled at least until April 30th.

Slovakia

All floorball events and leagues have been cancelled for this season.

Slovenia

All floorball events in Slovenia are currently suspended until further notice. The 3 nations (AUT, HUN, SLO) floorball league Final Four is postponed to May 1-17 (final decision will be taken in mid-April).

Spain

All floorball events and leagues have been cancelled until further notice.

Sweden

All floorball leagues and events are cancelled for this season. SSL regular season winners, Iksu women & Falun men, were crowned champions.

Switzerland

All floorball leagues and events are cancelled for this season.

Ukraine

All floorball events are cancelled until at least 3rd of April.

United Kingdom

English Floorball League season and 2020 National Finals cancelled.

IFF Events & Multi-sport Events

Since January 2020, COVID-19 has had an impact on the IFF Events and the IFF Executive Committee had to cancel the Men’s World Floorball Championships Qualification tournament in Bangkok, Thailand, based on the fact that Thailand was not in a position to organise the event. Read more here. On the 20th of March, the IFF ExCo also made the decision to postpone the Women’s U19 WFC 2020 to September.

The International University Sports Federation (FISU) has also cancelled the World University Floorball Championships 2020 planned to be held in Koper, Slovenia, from the 24th to 28th of June 2020 (The World University Championship Department will work on the possibility to postpone the event to the same period in 2021).

As the dates for the Olympics now clash with the World Games 2021, The World Games are urgently working on finding a new time period for The World Games 2021 in Birmingham.

Welcome To April! 70s Next Week?

We made it to April! April is the month we really get rid of winter and go right into Spring. While we do average near an inch of snowfall, April snow tends not to be a big deal. In fact, the last time we had more than a trace of snow in April was in 2018 with 3.2”.

April 2020 will start off with near normal temps today, but active weather will bring several chances for rain in the next two weeks as well as a warm up ahead of each system. We will be in the 60s Thursday, but next week will make run at the 70s on Tuesday!
