“Man uses estranged wife’s credit card for Vancouver-Mumbai trip, ordered by B.C. tribunal to pay back”

1 of 1 2 of 1

Singh Sushant and his wife Suhani Singh stopped living together in 2016.

In 2018, Sushant flew from Vancouver to Mumbai and back.

The flights were charged to the credit card of his estranged wife.

The woman claimed that her credit card was used by Sushant without her permission.

Sushant, for his part, suggested that the woman used her credit card to create “trouble” for him.

The estranged couple’s dispute was decided by a B.C. Civil Resolution Tribunal, which found the man’s argument not credible.

“I find it unlikely that Ms. Singh would book expensive flights to her own credit card in Mr. Singh’s name, only to make ‘trouble’ for him,” tribunal member Julie K. Gibson noted.

In her reasons for decision, Gibson stated that it is “unlikely for someone to incur several thousand dollars in personal credit card debt to make a point”.

Gibson noted that she considered the man’s submission that, because he no longer lived with Singh, he did not have access to her credit card.

“Because access to a physical credit card is not required for online purchases, I find that Mr. Singh could have made the bookings with only Ms. Singh’s credit card details,” according to Gibson.

Gibson found that Singh charged $2,798.31 to the woman’s credit card.

The woman had already received a $818.91 refund, but Singh must pay her the difference of $1,979.40.

Gibson ordered the man to pay back $1,979.40, plus $80.79 in interest and $125 in tribunal fees, for a total of $2,185.19.

“Retired Kerala police officer commits suicide after killing wife”

By IANS

THIRUVANANTHAPURAM: In a bizarre incident on Friday morning a retired Kerala Police official killed his wife, also a retired police officer and then committed suicide by hanging himself.

The incident happened in the capital city and the local police official said this happened around 8 a.m.

According to the local police official, who is probing the case, there were issues between the two.

“We received a call and we came and by that time everything had happened. We rushed his wife in an ambulance to the hospital. But she passed away,” said the local police official.

Seventy-year-old Ponnan had retired as an Additional Sub Inspector of police, while 68-year-old Leela also retired in the same post.

The neighbours also pointed out that there were skirmishes between the two.

As per the police, Ponnan had an argument with is wife in the morning and in a fit of rage hit Leela on her head with a log of wood following which she collapsed. Sensing she had died, he committed suicide by hanging himself.
