The new opening hours for Sainsbury's, Tesco, Aldi, Asda and other UK supermarkets

Since coronavirus hit the UK, there have been dozens of changes to how we shop.

From the initial panic-buying which saw limits introduced, to social distancing in queues and in store - something we once did without a second thought has now become full of rules and regulations to protect both supermarket staff and shoppers.

One thing which has changed multiple times is supermarket opening hours.

Initially many UK chains reduced their opening hours to allow their staff to restock the shelves safely.

Now some restrictions have been lifted, and so our sister title Essex Live has put together this handy guide on opening hours which we’ve updated with the latest information.

Aldi

Aldi opening hours have now been extended until 10pm Monday to Saturday, and normal Sunday hours are still in place.

They recommend that the quietest shopping hours are between 7pm and 10pm, whilst mornings remain very busy.

NHS workers and the police and fire service have priority access all day every day with valid ID.

Asda

Asda supermarkets will now open between 8am and 10pm Monday - Saturday, and normal Sunday opening hours will continue.

NHS, care home workers and carers are all able to priority shop between 8am and 9am Monday, Wednesday and Fridays.

Stores will also be open an hour earlier on Sundays for browsing for health workers.

Co-Op

Most Co-op stores are now open between 7am and 8pm Monday to Saturday.

Co-op petrol stations also remain open until 11pm.

Lidl

(Image: Manchester Evening News)

Most Lidl stores are now open between 8am and 10pm on weekdays and Saturdays.

On Sundays, they are open between 11am and 5pm.

Marks and Spencer

Opening times at M&S varied depending on the store, so you are best to check with your local.

Vulnerable and elderly customers get priority during the first hour of shopping on Monday and Thursday.

The same applies for NHS and emergency service workers on Tuesday and Friday.

Morrisons

Morrisons stores will now be open Monday to Saturday between 7am and 10pm.

On Sundays, the stores will open at 9.30am and close at 4pm.

NHS workers have exclusive opening hours between 6am and 7am every day apart from Sunday where it is 9am to 9.30am.

Sainsbury’s

(Image: AFP/Getty Images)

On Friday, Sainsbury’s announced major changes to its shopping hours and rules with new regulations around online deliveries as well.

In line with other major chains, Sainsbury’s cut its hours shorter when the UK went into lockdown - pulling 24-hour shopping at all its large stores and harmonising opening hours to 8pm closures.

But from Monday the vast majority of supermarkets will be open from 8am to 10pm - much later than has been the case for the last month.

Convenience stores will also extend their opening hours to 10pm or 11pm.

Tesco

Most large stores open between 6am and 7am until 10pm.

Tesco Express stores will open as normal but will also close at 10pm.

Special hours for the elderly and vulnerable are available at all superstores on Monday, Wednesday and Friday between 9am and 10am.

NHS workers also have the priority hour on Tuesdays and Thursdays, as well as a browsing hour every Sunday.

ANZAC Day trading hours: When Woolworths, Coles and Aldi are open

It will be an ANZAC Day like no other, with coronavirus social distancing restrictions meaning that Australians cannot attend traditional church services and marches to remember our war heroes.

But if you’re planning a commemorative ANZAC Day family dinner at home, you may need to plan ahead with the Anzac Day public holiday affecting trading hours at Woolworths, Coles and Aldi.

So what’s open in your state? Here’s our comprehensive guide to what supermarkets are open on April 25, 2020.

Woolworths*

  • Woolworths stores in NSW & ACT - open 9am to 6pm

  • Woolworths stores in Victoria - open 1pm to 10pm

  • Woolworths stores in Queensland & Western Australia - CLOSED**

  • Woolworth stores in South Australia - Metro stores open from 12pm to 5pm. All other stores closed.

  • Woolworths stores in NT - open 8am to 8pm

  • Woolworths stores in TAS - open 12:30pm to 10pm

Additionally, Woolworths’ fundraising support for the RSL ANZAC Appeal continues this year with all stores nationwide selling commemorative lapel pins, available from the customer service desk. Customers can also continue to donate directly to the RSL ANZAC Appeal at any register or when shopping online.

  • The above are general indicators only and may be subject to individual store opening and closing hours. Woolworths encourages customers to check the ANZAC Day opening hours of their local Woolworths by visiting woolworths.com.au/updates.

** In Queensland, the Brisbane airport store will be open from 1pm - 6pm.

Coles

  • Coles stores in NSW & ACT - open 9am to 8pm

  • Coles stores in Victoria - open 1pm to 8pm

  • Coles stores in Queensland - CLOSED

  • Coles stores in South Australia - CBD and suburban stores open from 12pm to 5pm; Mount Gambier, Port Lincoln, Port Pirie, Berri, Port Augusta, Mount Barker, Whyalla, Murray Bridge and Victor Harbor stores open from 12pm to 8pm.

  • Coles stores in Western Australia - Albany store open from 12pm to 5pm; Tom Price store open from 12pm to 6pm; Margaret River, Karratha, Chinatown, Kununurra, South Hedland and Eaton open from 12pm to 8pm; Vasse, Busselton, Dunsborough, Busselton Central open from 1pm to 8pm. All other stores closed.

  • Coles stores in NT - Alice Springs open 12pm to 9pm, all other stores open from 12pm to 8pm.

  • Coles stores in Tasmania - open 12:30pm to 8pm

Says a Coles spokesperson: “We work closely with government health authorities on the best measures to keep customers and our team members safe during the COVID-19 pandemic. Their continued advice is that social distancing remains an important measure to reduce the rate of transmission in the community.

‘We are grateful to state governments that have provided exemptions for trading on public holidays, which will assist in maintaining social distancing as customers have more options on when they do their shopping this weekend.”Coles has also launched a fundraising campaign in the lead up to ANZAC Day to enable Bravery Trust to support Aussie service men and women who have an injury or illness as a result of their service.

Until stores close on Sunday 26 April, Coles will donate 50 cents from every pack of Coles Bakery branded cookies or biscuits sold at Coles to the Bravery Trust. Customers are also welcome to make their own donations in-store at the checkout.

ALDI

An ALDI spokesperson tells New Idea Food that customers should check their local store’s trading hours on their website, as they vary from state to state.

‘This ANZAC Day, Saturday 25 April, selected ALDI stores will be closed or trading with amended opening hours. For stores that will be closed this ANZAC Day, Special Buys will be available on Sunday 26 April,’ says the spokesperson.

‘We always encourage customers to visit the ALDI website closer to the day for information about their local store’s trading hours.’

New shopping rules and opening hours at Tesco, Asda, Aldi, Sainsbury’s, NHS hours and item restrictions in full

Here are the latest rules for shopping at the major supermarkets.

When is Aldi open, what are its opening hours and what are Aldi’s shopping rules?

Aldi stores are now open up to 10pm every day, except for on Sundays when the times are 10am to 4pm.

There is an elderly and vulnerable shopping hour every 30 minutes from 9.30am every Monday to Saturday.

All stores will open half an hour early on Sundays for NHS, police and fire service workers or anyone with a Blue Light card.

If you’re eligible, you will need to bring a form of ID.

The number of people in store is still being restricted and there are markers on the floor. Aldi is encouraging people to shop alone where possible.

When is Asda open, what are its store opening hours and what are Asda’s shopping rules?

Asda opening times are now 8am to 10pm Monday to Saturday for the majority of stores. Sunday times will stay the same.

You can currently buy a maximum of three items for certain handwash and baby formula products and up to three of each item on all online orders.

There are no special opening times for the elderly or vulnerable but there is a volunteer shopping card so people can buy goods for those who can’t leave the house.

NHS and care workers can go to larger stores between 8am and 9am Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays and 9am-10am for browsing on Sundays.

The number of shoppers in the store at a time is still being managed and there is a no-touch rule, with the store stating ‘only pick up what you intend to buy’.

When is Morrisons open, what are its store opening hours and shopping rules?

Morrisons is now open 7am to 10pm Monday to Saturday, while Sunday opening hours are 9.30am to 4pm.

Morrisons has lifted in-store restrictions on number of items per customer.

Morrisons is also opening exclusively for NHS workers from 6-7am Monday to Saturday and 9.30am on Sunday.

When is Sainsbury’s open, what are its store opening hours and shopping rules?

Sainsbury’s is now open from 8am to 8pm Monday to Saturday while Sunday opening hours will remain the same.

Customers are able to buy up to three of any grocery item and a maximum of two toilet rolls, soap and long-life milk.

Every Sainsbury’s store has a dedicted 8am-9am slot for elderly, vulnerable and disabled customers and their carers.

Only one adult per household is being allowed into the store. Children are only welcome if they cannot be left at home.

There is also a queue system and markings on the shop floor.

When is Tesco open, what are its store opening hours and shopping rules?

Tesco has changed all its stores to 10pm closing but some Tesco branches can differ. There is a limit of 80 items per online order and restrictions on toilet roll, pasta and handwash products.

All stores will prioritise the elderly and most vulnerable between 9am and 10am every Monday, Wednesday and Friday except at Express stores.

NHS workers can shop exclusively at non-Express stores between 9am and 10am on Tuesdays and Thursdays.

Tesco is encouraging people to shop with ‘no more than one other person’ and there are floor markings in store and in car parks.

When is Waitrose open, what are its store opening hours and shopping rules?

Waitrose has not changed store opening hours - it never tended to go for late openings or 24 hours before anyway - but all branches have been given the option to be flexible with their hours and some may close early.
