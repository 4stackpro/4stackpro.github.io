Ajax footballer Abdelhak Nouri awakes from coma after 32 months

Abdelhak Nouri, a midfielder for Dutch football giants Ajax, has woken from a coma after two years, eight months and 19 days.

The player’s brother Abderrahim said the 22-year-old woke up and showed signs of life, Dutch daily De Telegraaf reported late on Thursday.

“It is going well with Appie [Abdelhak]. Yes, what is good? He’s awake, he’s asleep, he’s eating, he’s burping, but he’s not getting out of bed,” Abderrahim told the Amsterdam-based newspaper.

“At good times there is a form of communication, then he moves his eyebrows. He just can’t keep it up for long, that seems to be top sport for him,” he said, adding that Nouri was at home with his family and “aware of where he is”.

Cardiac arrest

The Dutch-Moroccan midfielder collapsed during a friendly match between Ajax and Germany’s Werder Bremen on July 8, 2017 in Austria due to a cardiac arrest, which caused serious brain damage.

Amsterdam-based Ajax were later forced to admit that their on-field treatment of the player was inadequate. Club doctor Don de Winter was fired.

Winter is believed to have deviated from UEFA’s guidelines, beginning resuscitation attempts too late. Ajax Chief Executive Edwin van der Sar said at the time a defibrillator should “have been used sooner”.

“Had this happened, it’s possible that Abdelhak would have come out in a better condition,” Van der Sar had said in a statement.

“This isn’t certain, but it’s a possibility.

“We recognise our responsibility and liability for the consequences of this.”

Ajax to ‘offer Abdelhak Nouri’s family £4.5m’ for ‘inadequate treatment’ but they ‘demand more’ after he awoke from coma – The Sun

AJAX are reportedly set to offer Abdelhak Nouri’s family £4.5million in compensation after admitting to “inadequate” treatment.

The 22-year-old was placed in an induced coma after he suffered a cardiac arrhythmia attack on the pitch in July 2017.

Nouri’s brother confirmed this week that he is out of the coma and able to communicate with family after going home.

Journalist Valentijn Driessen has claimed a compensation fee has not been agreed between the club and the player’s family.

It is reported that the Dutch side are considering an offer of £4.5m.

But Driessen allegedly added: “Nouri’s family want to have significantly more.

In the summer of 2018, Ajax admitted to providing “inadequate” care on the pitch after Nouri collapsed.

He was revived and airlifted to hospital, but suffered brain damage as a result of the attack.

At the time, Ajax chief Edwin van der Sar said: “We recognise our responsibility and liability for the consequences of this.

“For a long time we were convinced that Abdelhak had received the best possible care on the field.”

Demba Ba was among the long-list of players that paid tribute to the talented Dutchman this week.
