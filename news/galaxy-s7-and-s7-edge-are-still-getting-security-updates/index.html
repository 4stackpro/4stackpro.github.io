Galaxy S7 and S7 edge are still getting security updates

It was almost exactly four years ago that the Galaxy S7 and Galaxy S7 edge went on sale in markets around the world. The S7 and S7 edge stopped getting major Android updates with Android 8.0 Oreo, in keeping with Samsung’s software update policy for Galaxy devices. However, the Korean giant continues to support the Galaxy S7 and S7 edge with security updates to this day, and it has released the March security patch for the two phones in quite a few countries in the last week or so.

The Galaxy S7 and Galaxy S7 edge continue to be in the quarterly schedule for security updates four years after launch, though there’s a good chance that will change starting next month. That’s because Samsung’s policy dictates that every flagship get monthly security updates for three years and once every three months in the fourth year. From the fifth year onward, security updates only arrive when a critical security vulnerability is discovered and needs fixing.

The March 2020 security update is rolling out for the Galaxy S7 and S7 edge in a slew of markets around the world, and you can find out if it’s available in your country from the phone’s Settings » Software update menu. You can also check the Galaxy S7/S7 edge section in our firmware archive, where you can also download the latest firmware to manually install it on your device.

Galaxy S7 e S7 Edge recebem o último pacote de segurança de suas vidas

Já faz quase quatro anos que a Samsung começou a vender o Galaxy S7 e S7 Edge globalmente. Mesmo assim, há poucas semanas os dois modelos receberam o pacote de segurança com todas as proteções do Android até 1° de março de 2020, o que faz deles mais seguros que muitos intermediários mais recentes.

Pode parecer algo curioso, mas é uma política da Samsung. Os flagships da empresa são atualizados com duas novas versões do Android. Quando completam três anos do lançamento, começam a receber pacotes de segurança mensais, que se tornam trimestrais ao completar quatro anos de início das vendas. No quinto, passam a receber apenas updates críticos.

Tela do que deve ser o último update programado do Galaxy S7 (Foto: Reprodução/SamMobile)

Isso é o que deve acontecer agora com o Galaxy S8. O dispositivo não vai ser atualizado para o Android 10, mas entra na lista de modelos que recebem pacotes de segurança mensais até abril do ano que vem, quando passa a ser atualizado a cada três meses. E aí, em abril de 2022, passa a ser contemplado apenas com updates críticos, caso necessário.

S7 Airlines Giving People Miles For Staying Home

While there are lots of ways to earn miles from home (without flying), S7 Airlines is quite literally giving people miles for staying home. For those of you not familiar with S7, they’re a fascinating Russian airline that’s also a member of the oneworld alliance.

With S7’s “Fly At Home” promotion, the company is offering members 100 S7 Priority miles for every day that they confirm they’re at home.

The promotion is valid from March 30 through April 30, 2020, and you can earn 100 miles each day just for clicking a button that confirms you’re at home — so you can earn a total of 3,000(ish) miles through this promotion.

As the promotion page explains (via Google Translate):

The best that each of us can do in the fight against the pandemic today is to limit our movements and stay at home. We believe that travel will return to our lives again. Perhaps not now, but later, it is obligatory. We support everyone who respects quarantine and earn miles for every day spent at home. Confirm your presence at home by clicking the “I’m home” button on this page. And get 100 S7 Priority miles every day – they will come in handy for your future travels.

Bottom line

Well played, S7. What a cute promotion to send the right message while also engaging members in the program on a daily basis.
