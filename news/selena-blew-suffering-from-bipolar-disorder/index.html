Selena Gomez Just Revealed Her Bipolar Diagnosis

Selena Gomez recently revealed that she has been diagnosed with bipolar disorder.

The 27-year-old revealed her diagnosis while speaking to Miley Cyrus on her Brightminded Instagram Live show on Friday (April 3).

The “Lose You To Love Me” singer has been vocal about her own personal experiences with anxiety and mental health along with advocating for mental health.

“You know, I had gone to treatment a few times for anxiety and for depression, and for other stuff that I had been struggling with,” Gomez told Cyrus.

“Recently, I went to one of the best mental hospitals in the world … I discussed that after years of going through a lot of different things, I realized that I was bipolar,” she announced. “And so, when I got to know more information, it actually [helped] me. It doesn’t scare me once I know it. And I think people get scared of that, right?”

Gomez compared her experience to when she was scared of thunderstorms as a child. “My mom bought me all these books on thunderstorms… She was like, ‘The more you educate yourself on this, the more that you’re not going to be afraid,’” she explained. “It completely worked. That’s something that helps me big time.”

Fans have shown an outpouring of support for Gomez being so open about her mental health struggles.

MAC’s Selena La Reina Makeup Collection Launches in April — Details, Interview

With so much world news to follow right now, we almost — almost — forgot about what’s sure to be one of our favorite beauty events of the year. On February 25, MAC dropped the news of the brand’s second collaboration with the Quintanillas, the family of the late, great singer Selena. Now, we have all the details about the Selena La Reina collection, which translates, fittingly, into Selena the Queen.

Just like its predecessor, this Selena-inspired MAC makeup collection was created with plenty of collaboration from Selena’s family, especially her sister Suzette Quintanilla. “Clearly, this line is a celebration of Selena’s legacy. This is for her fans,” Quintanilla tells Allure.

“Clearly when you think about Selena, you think about her red lipstick and winged eyeliner,” Quintanilla says. “She also took it to another level with her style, and being able to be who she was as a Mexican-American. She took the era that we grew up in — Madonna, Jody Watley, Pebbles, Janet Jackson — and made it her own.”

With influences from Selena and all her influences, this many-piece makeup collection captures the essence of the Queen of Tejano music. The range includes red lipstick, of course, plus more lipsticks, lip pencil, lip gloss, liquid lipstick, finishing powder, an eye shadow palette, and the makeup bag to store it all in. Selena fans will recognize shade names from her song lyrics. Then there’s the superfan packaging, which includes a holographic photograph of Selena and her rose logo embossed into the makeup itself. “The packaging is just fun,” laughs Quintanilla.

First, let’s break down all those lip products. The range includes four lipstick bullets ($20 each) in Queen of Cumbia (matte red), Inolvidable (frosty peach), La Reina (brown), and Selena Vive (light pinky nude). You can complete that lip look with one of two pencils ($19.50), Selenaville (scarlet red) or Entre A Mi Mundo (brown nude) — or choose a Lipglass, MAC’s word for lip gloss, instead.

We’ve got three shades of pearlescent lipglass ($19) in pink, brown, or white shimmer. Finally (for the lip products, at least) there are three Retro Matte Liquid Lip Colour options ($23) in Siempre Selena (blue-hued red), Dame Un Beso (bright pink), and Big Bertha (dark brown).

As for face products, there’s an all-purpose Skinfinish Powder ($37). Use the nude gold powder as a highlighter, contour powder, or for all-over shimmer.

An eight-shade eye shadow palette ($35) completes the range. “The eye shadow colors were picked to have a wide variety of representation of whoever you are and what you like to wear,” says Quintanilla. The shades include matte and shimmer options in pinks, nudes, and bronzes.
