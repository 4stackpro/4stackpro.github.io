Into The Wild with Bear Grylls and Rajinikanth registers second highest ratings in the genre

Superstar Rajinikanth made his television debut after 43 years with Into The Wild with Bear Grylls. The primiere episode of the reality television show featuring Rajinikanth aired on March 23. According to the latest reports, the show has registered the second highest ratings in the genre with over 4 million impressions.

The show aired on the Discovery Network (12 channels) and was regarded as the highest-rated show this year and second highest-rated show in the history of reality shows.

According to BARC India, the cumulative reach of the premiere show is 12.4 million, which is 86 per cent higher than the past four weeks. Reportedly, the viewership for the particular time slot grew four times higher in the last four weeks.

Into The Wild with Bear Grylls and Rajinikanth was simultaneously aired in Tamil in Discovery Tamil channel. The BARC India readings show a huge spike in viewership - 20 times higher than usual - and pushed other channels, like Colours Tamil, Raj TV and Jaya, behind during the slot.

Rajinikanth suffered minor injuries while shooting for the show in Badipur Tiger Reserve forest. He said, “I have completed the shooting of an episode of Man vs Wild. I did not suffer any major injury but just scratches due to little thorns, that’s it. I am alright.”

Last year, Prime Minister Narendra Modi featured in Man Vs Wild along with Bear Grylls. The show registered the highest ratings in 2019 with 3.05 million impressions.

Coronavirus: Twitter removes Rajinikanth’s video

Twitter on Saturday night removed a video posted by Tamil superstar Rajinikanth, in which he says that the spread of COVID-19 can be prevented if people don’t step out for 14 hours, for violating its rules.

The action by the micro-blogging site came after several Twitter users “reported” the tweet saying Rajinikanth’s claims were “misleading” and would send a wrong message to the people on preventing spread of coronavirus.

The video was removed from the Twitter page of Rajinikanth, who is followed by 5.7 million people, at around 8 pm on Saturday, four hours after it was posted. Twitter said the tweet violated its rules in its explanation for removing the video.

However, the YouTube link for the video was part of the tweet till the time of going to press. The tweet was liked by 10,500 people and retweeted by more than 3,600 Twitter users.

In the video, Rajinikanth had said that COVID-19 spread can be prevented if people don’t step out for 14 hours. He also added this was the reason Prime Minister Narendra Modi had called for a 14-hour self-imposed curfew in the country.

“Right now, India is in the second stage of battling the coronavirus and we can’t let it go to the third level. If we can prevent the spread of the COVID-19 in public spaces for 12 to 14 hours, that is enough to stop it from progressing to the third stage,” the actor had said.
