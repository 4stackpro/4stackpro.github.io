Apple TV+推原創卡通《Central Park》，Fiona Apple、Cyndi Lauper、Meghan Trainor加入原聲帶作曲陣容！

蘋果公司的串流媒體平台 Apple TV+ 原計畫今年3月要在SXSW音樂節發布原創音樂動畫《Central Park（中央公園）》，因為武漢肺炎疫情延到5月29日才釋出第一、二集節目。隨著前兩集的播出，《Central Park》的原聲帶歌曲已陸續數位發行，整季節目的作曲名單也出爐，當中可以看到Fiona Apple、Cyndi Lauper等大牌唱作人加入作曲陣容！

《Central Park》是Apple TV+的全新原創音樂動畫喜劇，由知名動畫《Bob’s Burgers（開心漢堡店）》的作者Loren Bouchard，以及Nora Smith和喜劇演員Josh Gad共同創作。故事圍繞長居在美國紐約中央公園的一個家庭，成員中的Owen是中央公園的管理人員，他的太太Paige是一名記者，兩人還有一對子女Molly和Cole；他們一家肩負守護公園的責任，避免這塊綠洲落入女富豪Bitsy Brandenham的手中而變成商業開發區。

第一季《Central Park》共10集、每集片長半小時，頭兩集節目於5月29日上線後，每周五都會推出新的一集。與此同時，動畫的配樂及原聲帶也已經數位發行，整季的歌曲創作者隨之揭曉，像是Fiona Apple、Cyndi Lauper、Aimee Mann、Sara Bareilles和Meghan Trainor等知名歌手都為《Central Park》創作原創音樂。其他作曲人還包括Anthony Hamilton、Darren Criss、Rafael Casal，以及Utkarsh Ambudkar等演員和歌手。

雖然他們目前沒有在《Central Park》劇中獻聲，不過每一集節目都將有3到6首原創歌曲，並由Josh Gad、Leslie Odom, Jr.、Kristen Bell、Kathryn Hahn、Tituss Burgess、Daveed Diggs，以及Stanley Tucci等大咖配音卡司們獻唱。

根據Variety報導，1980年代傳奇歌手Cyndi Lauper為第4集節目創作了《Garage Ballet》和《Rats》兩首歌曲；Aimee Mann創作的《Big Deal》出現在第7集；Meghan Trainor寫的《I’m in a Perfect Releationship》能在第8集聽到；而由Fiona Apple共同創作的歌曲《New York Doesn’t Like Your Face》則會在最後第10集節目亮相。

美國歌手及鋼琴家Sara Bareilles為《Central Park》創作的〈Weirdos Make Great Superheroes〉一曲由知名演員Kristen Bell與Tituss Burgess於第2集劇中演唱，官方已在YouTube釋出歌詞版的影片；更多原聲帶歌曲則可在Apple Music或Spotify等串流平台上收聽。

“Nicki Minaj: How Cyndi Lauper Influenced Her Style”

Cyndi Lauper is one of the most enduringly popular singers of the 1980s. Many modern pop singers seem to take inspiration from her. Nicki Minaj has released numerous pop songs but they generally don’t sound like Lauper’s 1980s hits.

Despite this, Minaj said Lauper influenced her. Specifically, Minaj said Lauper helped her to develop her iconic fashion sense.

Nicki Minaj | OLI SCARFF/AFP via Getty Images

How Cyndi Lauper inspired Nicki Minaj to dye her hair

Minaj is known for her over-the-top sense of fashion. She’s worked everything from Hello Kitty dolls to horse headdresses. In addition, she regularly changes the color of her hair. Minaj sees Lauper as one of her fashion inspirations, noting that she decided to change her hair color as a teenager because she saw Lauper’s music videos.

According to Contact Music, Minaj opened up about her interest in dying her hair. She said she’s “always been intrigued by color and by interesting hair. I was one of those weird little girls doing my own hair at the age of nine… [and] getting weird gels and new brushes and cornrow holders. I would tweak and perm at the age of 13.”

Nicki Minaj | Todd Oren/Getty Images for Audi

Minaj discusses the first time she got her hair colored and how Lauper helped inspire her to do so. “When I first went to get my hair colored, I was about 14 and I wanted blonde highlights. The beautician said, ‘No, you have to get your mother on the phone,’ and I was just crying and begging. I’ve always been experimenting. Cyndi Lauper’s videos – that’s what intrigued me.”

In addition, Minaj explained why she likes to present herself in a unique way. “I like the idea of doing something that everyone else is not. I get high off that. Just the idea that other people don’t have the balls to do something – that’s my thing.”

Other ways Nicki Minaj and Cyndi Lauper have artistically crossed paths

Katy Perry and Nicki Minaj’s cover of “Girls Just Wanna Have Fun”

RELATED: Nicki Minaj: What Happened the Time She Got in a Feud With Cher

Minaj has referenced Lauper in her work multiple times. MTV reports Minaj and Katy Perry covered Lauper’s “Girls Just Wanna Have Fun” at VH1 Divas in 2010. According to Rolling Stone, Minaj later paid tribute to Lauper by reinterpreting Lauper’s classic hit “True Colors” with Wiz Khalifa. Their version of the track is a trap song that is very different than Lauper’s pop ballad, but it was still inspired by Lauper’s song.

What does Lauper think of Minaj? According to Singersroom, Lauper expressed admiration for Minaj and some of her fellow rappers, like Lil Wayne and Snoop Dogg. “I like Nicki Minaj a lot. My son went through all the hip hop stuff with me and he showed me that 50’s voice is kind of butter smooth.”

Minaj and Lauper might not make music that sounds similar. However, Lauper has clearly impacted Minaj’s aesthetics and art. In turn, Lauper sees value in Minaj’s music and praised her vocals.

“Cyndi Lauper explica admiração por Billie Holiday e assume que quer ver Donald Trump fora da Casa Branca”

Não lança nenhum disco há quatro anos mas, nas redes sociais, continua muito ativa. Nos últimos dias, a irreverente cantora norte-americana Cyndi Lauper, que fez furor na década de 1980 com sucessos internacionais como “Time after time”, “Girls just want to have fun” e “True colors”, atualmente com 66 anos, prestou, no âmbito do Mês da Música Negra, homenagem aos artistas afro-americanos com quem trabalhou ao longo da carreira e aos que mais as inspiravam antes de ser famosa.

“Eu gravei muita música negra. Muita mesmo! E tenho sempre a Billie Holiday a olhar por mim”, afirma Cyndi Lauper, que tem um retrato da cantora pendurado na sala onde colocou o piano que usa para tocar e para compor. “Ela mudou a minha vida quando eu era uma jovem cantora que ainda ninguém tinha ouvido”, que elaborou uma lista na plataforma digital Spotify que reúne algumas das versões de canções de artistas afro-americanos que gravou e os originais que foram lançados.

Dias antes, para assinalar o Mês do Orgulho LGBT [sigla de lésbicas, gays, bissexuais, transexuais], Cyndi Lauper publicou, nas redes sociais, um vídeo de apoio a esta comunidade, com uma mensagem que inclui um apelo. “Continuaremos a lutar e a celebrar. E, por favor, certifiquem-se que estão inscritos [nos cadernos eleitorais] para votar para que possamos, todos, dar a volta a isto em novembro”, pediu. Uma crítica implícita a Donald Trump, que se recandidatou à Casa Branca.
