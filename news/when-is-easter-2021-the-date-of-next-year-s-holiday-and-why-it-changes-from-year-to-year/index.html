When is Easter 2021? The date of next year’s holiday, and why it changes from year to year

Easter 2021 falls just over a week earlier than this year’s date, which should fall towards the beginning of the school holidays

By Serina Sandhu Saturday, 11th April 2020, 8:30 am Updated Saturday, 11th April 2020, 1:25 pm

The date of Easter changes from year to year, which means that the holidays can sneak up on you - so to ensure you’re prepared here’s when it falls in 2021, and how the process of calculating Easter Sunday’s date works.

When is Easter 2021?

In 2021, Easter Sunday falls on on Sunday 4 April, just over a week earlier than this year’s date of 12 April.

For most people in the UK, this should fall towards the beginning of the school holidays, whereas this year’s celebrations should have been in the middle weekend of the break.

The holiday can take place on any date between 22 March and 25 April, with Easter last year falling on 21 April, the latest since 2011.

The position of Easter in the calendar depends on when the Spring Equinox falls (Photo: Getty Images)

How do we work out when Easter will be?

The date of Easter is calculated from the first Sunday after the first full moon following the Spring equinox in March.

“The reason for this is that Easter must occur after the biblical festival of Passover, on the full moon, when Jesus was crucified,” said Professor Sacha Stern, head of the Hebrew and Jewish Studies department at University College London.

The decision on how and when Easter should fall each year was made by the Council of Nicaea in 325 AD, the first major church council.

Why did the Council of Nicaea need to intervene? As the crucifixion and resurrection of Jesus happened after Passover, some early Christians decided to celebrate it then - on the 14th of the month of Nisan (from the Assyrian and Hebrew calendars). This correlates with March or April in the Gregorian calendar (named after Pope Gregory XIII), which is what we use today. Other early Christians preferred to celebrate on a Sunday because it is thought Jesus’s tomb was found on this day, according to Brent Landau, a lecturer in religious studies at the University of Texas. The Council of Nicaea was asked to resolve this. It decided Easter should be after the first full moon following the March equinox.

Easter can take place on any date between 22 March and 25 April (Photo: Taronga Zoo via Getty Images)

Why is Easter on a different date each year?

The predominant reason why Easter falls on a different date each year is because we now use the solar, Gregorian calendar rather than a lunar one. This means the full moon occurs on different dates each year, and therefore so does Easter.

Dr Greg Brown, astronomer at the Royal Observatory Greenwich, said Easter is based on a combination of the seven-day week and the cycle of the phases of the Moon.

“The March equinox is the date when the sun crosses from the southern hemisphere of the sky to the northern hemisphere marking the beginning of spring.

“The day and night of the equinox are of approximately equal length. As neither the calendar year (365 days) nor the cycle of the phases of the Moon (29.5 days) divide evenly by the seven-day week, the date of Easter Sunday can move irregularly by up to a month, from between late March and late April.”

Occasionally Orthodox churches celebrate Easter on the same date as Catholics and other western churches (Photo: Getty Images)

Why do different churches celebrate Easter on different days?

It’s down to using different calendars.

Eastern Churches (Greek and Slavic) and Oriental Churches (Syrian, Armenian, Coptic Egyptian and Ethiopian) continued using the Julian Calendar, named after Julius Caesar, even after Europe adopted the Gregorian Calendar in 1582.

“This is why even now Easter is calculated differently by the Eastern and Oriental Orthodox Churches to the Catholic and other western Churches,” said theology and religion Professor Emma Loosely, from the University of Exeter.

“Easter is only ever celebrated by all Christians on the relatively rare occasions when the two calendars align,” she added.

Easter Sunday 2020: What’s open, what’s closed on Sunday? Restaurants, grocery stores, pharmacies, banks, UPS, mail delivery

Easter Sunday 2020 is on April 12.

It is a sacred, religious holiday that celebrates the belief in the resurrection of Jesus Christ. Popular traditions include the tale of the Easter Bunny, attending church and egg hunts.

Select stores and services are closed, some indefinitely due to the coronavirus. Many churches are also planning to livestream mass on the holiday.

Here is everything you need to know about what’s open and what’s closed on Easter Day 2020.

Is Easter a federal holiday?

Easter is neither a federal nor state holiday. The next federal holiday is Memorial Day, on May 25.

Are restaurants open on Easter Sunday?

Some local restaurants may be offering takeout and delivery, but none in New Jersey will be offering dine-in services due to the coronavirus.

It is best to call ahead and check with your local restaurants.

What fast-food restaurants are open on Easter Sunday? Is McDonald’s open? Burger King? Starbucks?

Most McDonald’s locations will be open on Easter, according to MyRecipes.com. However, the fast-food chain is only offering drive-thru, takeout and delivery services due to the coronavirus. Make sure to check the store locator for local hours.

Same goes for Burger King. Check the store locator for hours.

Some Starbucks locations also will be open. It varies by location, so it’s best to call your local Starbucks and ask.

Panera Bread has many of its locations across the country open for business on Easter.

You can also try diner-style chains like Denny’s and IHOP.

MyRecipes has compiled a list of 30 Restaurants That’ll Be Open For Takeout On Easter, with some varying by location.

Are malls open on Easter Sunday?

No, not in New Jersey. Although malls usually stay open on Easter Sunday, all New Jersey malls are closed indefinitely due to the coronavirus.

Are grocery stores open on Easter Sunday?

Select supermarkets will remain open or have reduced hours, and some will be closed to give workers a break to spend time with family. Shoppers should ultimately check with their local supermarket for specific information.

Will any other retailers that are considered essential be closed on Easter Sunday?

Lowe’s announced that all of its stores and distribution centers in the U.S. and Canada will be closed on Easter.

CEO Marvin Ellison said in the statement he wants employees “to spend Easter Sunday with their families and loved ones, and recharge.”

Is the post office open on Easter Sunday?

No. United States Postal Service offices are usually closed every Sunday, regardless if it’s a holiday or not.

Is there mail delivery on Easter Sunday? Will the mail run?

There will be no regular mail delivered on Easter. Only Priority Mail Express and Amazon packages (Prime members only) are delivered on Sundays.

UPS will not be delivering (except for UPS Express Critical).

FedEx’s Home Delivery will have modified services, but all other delivery will be closed (except for FedEx Custom Critical).

Does NJ Transit, PATH and SEPTA operate on a normal schedule?

NJ Transit, PATH and SEPTA are operating on reduced schedules amid the coronavirus.

For NJ Transit, riders should use NJ Transit’s online trip planner to double-check arrival and departure times.

Are the stock markets open on Easter Sunday?

The New York Stock Exchange and Nasdaq trading are closed on the weekends, regardless if it’s a holiday or not.

Both have also shifted to online-trading amid the coronavirus.

Are New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission locations open on Easter Sunday?

All Motor Vehicle Commission offices are closed indefinitely amid the coronavirus. Additionally, all New Jersey state offices and courts are closed and have shifted to video and phone conferencing options.

Online processing of some documents are also available on Motor Vehicle Commission’s website.

When is the next federal holiday?

Memorial Day is the next federal holiday of the year, on May 25.
