Kingsman 3: Fortsetzung kommt nach dem Prequel

Das stammt abermals aus der Hand von Regisseur Vaughn und erzählt die Vorgeschichte der Agenten-Saga – allerdings mit ganz neuen Schauspielern. Da Fans aber vor allem an der Story um Eggsy und Harry Hart interessiert sind, werden die Rufe nach einer tatsächlichen Fortsetzung der “Kingsman”-Reihe immer lauter. Und auch diesen Wunsch erfüllt Matthew Vaughn seiner Anhängerschaft. “Kingsman 3” befindet sich bereits in der Planung. Einen Starttermin gibt es bislang aber ebenso wenig wie Details zur Story. Als sicher gilt jedoch, dass sich der dritte Teil der Reihe erneut der Beziehung zwischen Eggsy und Hart widmet. Im Gespräch mit einem Online-Magazin erklärte Matthew Vaughn, dass er die Story der beiden zu Ende erzählen wolle.

Kingsman 3: Start wohl erst 2022

Denkbar wäre, dass sich der Regisseur erneut bei einer der Comic-Vorlagen bedient, um die Serie weiterzuführen. Die bietet mit den Büchern “The Big Exit” und “The Red Diamond” noch jede Menge Potenzial für spannende Geschichten und verarbeitet unter anderem so aktuelle Themen wie den Brexit. Da der Stand der Produktion für “Kingsman 3” nicht bekannt ist, lässt sich aktuell kaum abschätzen, wann Sie mit der Fortsetzung rechnen können. Ein Kinostart scheint frühestens 2021 möglich, deutlich wahrscheinlicher ist aber ein Release im Jahr 2022.

‘Fortnite’ umbrella floats ‘Kingsman’ movies tribute

In a game known for its film tie-ins, a bullet-deflecting umbrella gives players a last-chance reprise ahead of 2020’s Kingsman movie prequel.

Rolled out as part of March 31’s Fortnite game update, a new piece of equipment called the Kingsman lets players float, swipe and deflect projectiles.

Just like in 20th Century Fox’s Kingsman: The Secret Service (2015) and Kingsman: The Golden Circle (2017), the Kingsman umbrella allows players to swipe and stab at players with melée attacks that appear equally derived from fencing and cricket.

The smart black personal canopy can be used to slow mid-air ascents and rotated at high speed to deflect incoming projectiles.

Fortnite has engaged in numerous official movie and comic book tie-ins, including those surrounding Marvel’s Avengers ensemble movies, DC Comics’ Batman Day, Disney’s Ralph Breaks The Internet and Lionsgate’s Keanu Reeves action movie franchise John Wick.

Notably, the John Wick character was part of Fortnite lore before Lionsgate’s involvement: a supremely similar costume (or “skin”) was introduced in February 2018.

Known as The Reaper, the limited availability look was swiftly dubbed The Wick, or simply John Wick, by the Fortnite community — at least up until an official John Wick character model, bearing greater likeness to film actor Keanu Reeves, went on sale through the in-game shop in May 2019.

Fortnite developer and publisher Epic Games has not published patch notes to explain its game updates since October 2019, when it rebooted the game as Fortnite: Chapter 2, so there’s no official commentary on the Kingsman or other additions in version 12.30.

However, the timing is good, as 20th Century Fox has provisionally dated franchise prequel The King’s Man for September 2020.

The Kingsman motif also dovetails nicely with a secret agent theme debuting as part of Fortnite Season 2: Chapter 2, which started February 20.

Epic and 20th Century Fox have already collaborated with the gradual introduction of superhero character Deadpool into the cross-platform console, computer and mobile game, also coinciding with the start of Chapter 2.

The free-to-play game is supported by optional seasonal passes and virtual item purchases. — AFP Relaxnews

A new Kingsman-inspired, bullet-blocking umbrella is coming to Fortnite

Since Fortnite’s current season theme is all about secret agents, you might’ve expected some quintessential gear like a laser watch would’ve been added. Nope, we’re getting a bulletproof umbrella instead.

Taken right out of the film Kingsman: The Secret Service, a new update has introduced the bulletproof umbrella seen the over-the-top Brit action flick. They can be found around the map and are a legendary-rank item for you to play around with.

It works exactly as it does in the film: hold the umbrella open towards a foe and their spray of bullets will block them for a time. The brolly can’t be used indefinitely, however, but it might be the difference between life and death in those clutch moments.

You can even go full Mary Poppins and use the umbrella as a temporary glider, and to whack people with as a melee weapon.

But watch out below, because you can only glide for a short amount of time and you may or may not fall to an untimely death, as the video below perfectly demonstrates.

This umbrella item arrived in the latest update, which went live yesterday. Another item of note added to the game was in the form of a crash pad, which inflates to prevent falling damage.

It’s the perfect companion to any short, flying umbrella trips you might plan on taking - just make sure you don’t miss as you come crashing back to the ground. Use them from any height, or as an escape if you need to jump off a high place while under pressure.

It’s unclear how long these items will last and how they will affect the current meta before they inevitably get vaulted in a future update.
