Bruce Willis and Demi Moore: A complete relationship timeline

Before Brad Pitt and Angelina Jolie and George and Amal Clooney, Bruce Willis and Demi Moore were Hollywood’s golden couple.

He was an action hero and she was a movie star, and together they were fire. But just like that, their flame burned out and they called it quits in 1998 after 11 years of marriage.

However, unlike some celebrity pairings, Willis, 65, and Moore, 57, remained good friends for the sake of their three daughters — the family is even in quarantine together, currently riding out the coronavirus pandemic by each other’s side.

Here’s a look back at the couple’s relationship, from how Willis wooed the actress to her very candid tell-all memoir…

Bruce Willis and Demi Moore went from lovers to exes to best friends. (Getty)

July, 1987: Just like many other Hollywood couples, Willis and Moore meet at a movie screening in LA. The actress accompanies her then-fiancé, Emilio Estevez, to the premiere of his film, Stakeout, and Willis is among the guests at the event.

“My impression was, he’s kind of a jerk… but Bruce was so gallant — in his own boisterous way, a real gentleman,” Moore reveals years later in her memoir, Inside Out.

In the tell-all, Moore remembers attending an afterparty with Willis that same night, where he requests she write her phone number on his arm. Later, when she drives home, the actor and his entourage pull up beside her car on a highway.

“It was a stretch limousine in the next lane, with Bruce Willis and his buddies poking up through the open sunroof, waving and shouting, ‘Hey, Demi!'” she writes. “It was like the universe was telling me: pay attention to this one.”

Moore and Estevez, her co-star in St. Elmo’s Fire, split soon after.

Demi Moore and Bruce Willis attend their first Emmy Awards as a couple in September 1987. (Ron Galella Collection via Getty)

November, 1987: The actress embarks on a relationship with Willis, who sweeps her off her feet, wining and dining her and taking her around on a private jet.

During one impromptu visit to Las Vegas, Willis proposes and Moore says ‘yes’. After a whirlwind four months of dating, the couple marry inside their suite at the Golden Nugget hotel, with only a handful of guests in attendance.

“We were moving to the gambling tables when Bruce said, ‘I think we should get married.’ We’d been joking about it on the flight there, but suddenly it didn’t seem like he was kidding,” she writes.

One month after their Vegas wedding, the couple have another ceremony, this time it is a lavish affair in front of their famous friends in LA. Singer Little Richard, an ordained minister, marries the couple.

This is Willis’ first marriage — Moore was married to singer Freddie Moore for five years before their 1985 split, but she held on to his surname.

The couple (seen here in 1988) marry in Las Vegas after a whirlwind four-month romance. (Getty)

The actress reportedly falls pregnant with her and Willis’ first child, Rumer, on their wedding night.

“We had a whirlwind, truncated infatuation that morphed into a full-on family, all in our very first year,” Moore recalls in her memoir. “When reality set in, I don’t know if we really knew each other.”

They welcomed their first daughter, Rumer, exactly nine months after their wedding. (Instagram)

1990s: Following Rumer’s birth in August 1988, the couple move to rural Idaho to raise their family away from the glare of Hollywood. They later welcome two more daughters, Scout in 1991 and Tallulah in 1994.

During this period, Willis and Moore juggle parenthood and their careers, regularly travelling to Hollywood from Idaho during filming.

Willis and Moore quickly became Hollywood’s golden couple in the ’90s. (WireImage)

In her memoir, Moore reveals that their busy schedules took a heavy toll on their marriage. But they forge on and become Hollywood’s golden couple.

Both are at the height of their careers — Willis stars in a slew of films such as Twelve Monkeys, Pulp Fiction and two more Die Hard movies, meanwhile Moore brings in the big bucks with Ghost, Indecent Proposal and A Few Good Men.

In 1991, they also embarked on their Planet Hollywood restaurant venture with fellow A-listers Sylvester Stallone and Arnold Schwarzenegger. (Getty)

Rumours begin to circulate that the couple are on the brink of divorce, with their busy schedules to blame. In 1993, two years after a pregnant Moore posed nude on the cover of Vanity Fair, she fatefully tells the magazine that “[the rumours] won’t stop until one day they may be right”.

Moore poses on the cover of Vanity Fair in 1991 while seven months pregnant with daughter Scout. (Vanity Fair)

It isn’t until Moore’s 2019 memoir that she confirms the rumours were true. The actress writes about Willis preferring that she stay home with the kids, saying of their marriage, “This is never going to work if you’re off shooting a film.”

“I experienced the most conventional family dynamic I’d ever known in those years,” she shares in her tell-all.

1996: Cheating allegations surround their marriage, and more eyebrows are raised when Willis shares his thoughts on monogamy in an interview with Playboy in 1996.

“No woman is going to satisfy a man’s natural impulse to procreate, procreate, procreate. The impulse doesn’t go away because you have three or 10 or a hundred kids,” he tells the magazine.

“We try hard, but we’re animals. We’re just donkeys walking up to the trough for food and wanting to f— everything we see because of this unconscious agenda.”

Moore later explains that Willis “craved excitement and novelty. Basically he wanted to do whatever the f— he wanted. He was 36, throw in celebrity and money. You do the math.”

The couple put on a brave face in 1996 despite cheating rumours surrounding their marriage. (Getty)

1998: In June 1998, the couple release a statement and go their separate ways.

“Bruce Willis and Demi Moore have announced they are ending their marriage after 11 years. They were married on November 21, 1987,” writes Paul Bloch, a spokesman for the actor.

According to reports, the couple have been living apart for months due to conflicting film schedules. After the announcement, Moore retreats from the spotlight to focus on raising their daughters. The couple’s divorce is finalised in 2000.

“It’s a funny thing to say, but I’m very proud of our divorce,” she later reveals. “I think Bruce was fearful at the beginning that I was going to make our split difficult, and that I would express my anger and whatever baggage that I had from our marriage by obstructing his access to the kids — that I’d turn to all of those ploys divorcing couples use as weapons. But I didn’t, and neither did he.”

Attending their last Emmy Awards as a couple in 1997. They announced their split nine months later. (WireImage)

2003: Moore returns to the spotlight to make a cameo in Charlie’s Angels: Full Throttle, her first appearance in a feature film in five years. She is now 41 years old and is the fittest she has been in her life, yet movie roles are far and few.

“They’d say they don’t really know what to do with you, where to place you,” she says. “I was like, oh, well is that supposed to flatter me?”

Nevertheless, her career experiences a resurgence of sorts and Moore is once again in demand. Around the same time, she finds love again with That ’70s Show star Ashton Kutcher, the pair meeting at a play in New York.

The actor is 25 years old but the 16-year age gap does not faze the couple, who publicly debut their relationship at the premiere of Charlie’s Angels: Full Throttle.

Moore with boyfriend Ashton Kutcher and her daughters arriving for the 2003 premiere of Charlie’s Angeles: Full Throttle. (Getty)

2005: After two years of dating, Moore and Kutcher marry in a private ceremony at their home in Beverly Hills on September 25. Only 100 of the couple’s nearest and dearest are invited to the event, including stars Wilmer Valderrama and Lucy Liu. Willis also attends the event.

The actress later refers to her relationship with her new husband as “a do-over” of her twenties.

“Like I could just go back in time and experience what it was like to be young, with him — much more so than I’d ever been able to experience it when I was actually in my twenties,” she reveals.

Moore describes her relationship with Kutcher as ‘a do-over’ of her twenties. (WireImage)

2009: Willis marries model Emma Heming, who is 23 years younger, in an intimate ceremony in Turks & Caicos on March 21. The newlyweds met through friends many years ago and dated for more than one year before tying the knot.

The private affair — which Moore attends with Kutcher — is held at Willis’ Parrot Cay estate overlooking the Caribbean.

“It was a tough time for me during those years after the break-up,” the actor tells W Magazine of his divorce in July. “I went from ‘F— love’ to ‘Love is truly the answer.’

“I spent the last 10 years single and, for the most part, unhappy. In a dark place. I never thought that being with someone else was the answer. I would say, ‘I’m alone, but I’m not lonely.’ But I was just kidding myself. Then I started hanging around Emma, and on a day-to-day basis my life became much happier.”

Willis and Emma Heming attend the Met Gala two months after their 2009 wedding. (FilmMagic)

2011: Moore and Kutcher announce their separation after six years of marriage.

“It is with great sadness and a heavy heart that I have decided to end my six-year marriage to Ashton,” Moore writes in a statement. “As a woman, a mother, and a wife there are certain values and vows that I hold sacred, and it is in this spirit that I have chosen to move forward with my life.”

Reports suggest the couple — who finalise their divorce in 2013 — split due to Kutcher’s alleged infidelity.

They never had children during their time together, but Moore later reveals that they miscarried six months into their first pregnancy, just before their 2005 wedding. She says they were going to have a daughter and her name was to be Chaplin Ray.

Kutcher and Moore attend his No Strings Attached movie premiere in January 2011. They split later that year. (Getty)

2015: Moore and Willis continue to co-parent their daughters since their divorce, with their eldest child, Rumer, praising their efforts.

“I never had to split up vacations or split up birthdays,” Rumer reveals on Larry King Now. “They always made an effort to do all of the family events still together and made such an effort to still have our family be as one unit, as opposed to two separate things, which I think really made an impact.”

They remain one big happy family, and when Willis welcomes children with new wife Heming — daughters Mabel, eight, and Evelyn, five — Rumer is the first to embrace her half-siblings.

“The four most important beings on this planet and in any year from now to eternity,” she gushes of her sisters on Instagram. “Thank you for making everyday better. For the tears, the belly hurting laughter, the inside jokes, cuddles, Netflix binges, secret language, surprise sleepovers, pushing me to grow, sharing clothes and for all that is to come. You are my best friends for life.”

Rumer is protective of her sisters, including her dad’s children with his second wife. (Instagram)

2018: The Comedy Central Roast of Bruce Willis airs on TV and Moore is one of the stars to playfully throw shade at the actor.

Taking to the stage to roast her ex, Moore proves that they’ve both come a long way since their divorce, and are now able to laugh about their failed marriage.

Moore speaks onstage during the Comedy Central Roast of Bruce Willis. (Getty)

“For those of you that don’t know me, I am Demi Moore. I was married to Bruce for the first three Die Hard movies, which makes sense because the last two sucked,” she tells the audience as the actor sits nearby.

Later, she makes another dig, saying, “I look at our marriage like The Sixth Sense. You were dead the whole time.”

Sharing a laugh at the afterparty for the Comedy Central Roast of Bruce Willis. (Getty)

March, 2019: Willis renews his vows with Heming at his Parrot Cay home in Turks & Caicos, the same place they married 10 years ago.

“We said, we do, again, 10 years later,” Heming writes on Instagram while sharing a photo of their big day.

Moore attends the wedding and features in a happy family portrait eldest daughter Rumer shares on Instagram.

“Papa and Stepmama got married again 10 years later!!! So happy I was able to be here and celebrate with the crew. I love you both so much,” Rumer captions the photo.

Moore sits far left at the vow renewal of Willis and Heming in 2019. (Instagram)

September, 2019: Moore releases her memoir, Inside Out, in which she confesses she was once not a great mother or wife. While she is in a better place now with her daughters, she writes that it was a long road to rebuilding their relationships.

Moore also details her substance abuse issues and breakups with both Willis and Kutcher. The Die Hard star supports his ex’s book and even attends the launch in LA with Heming. Willis also calls Moore personally to tell her he is ‘proud’ of her memoir.

“I was getting ready to do Jimmy Fallon Live the other night, and I was in the dressing room and my phone rang, and it was Bruce,” she reveals in a Netflix podcast while promoting the book. “I was prepared for there, maybe, to be some sensitivities, and he called and he got very emotional. He said, ‘I’m so proud of you.'”

Moore’s daughters, ex-husband Willis and his new wife (second from right) at the actress’ book launch in 2019. (Getty)

Kutcher, however, was not so pleased that Moore shared details of their marriage in the book, where she brought forth allegations of threesomes and infidelity.

“Because we had brought in a third party into our relationship, Ashton said, that blurred the lines and, to some extent, justified what he’s done,” Moore writes of his alleged cheating.

After hearing of the contents of the book, Kutcher tweets, “I was about to push the button on a really snarky tweet. Then I saw my son, daughter, and wife and I deleted it.”

The actor is now married to That ’70s Show co-star Mila Kunis and together they share two children, Wyatt, five, and Dimitri, three.

The actress details her marriages to Willis and Kutcher in her 2019 memoir, Inside Out. (Harper)

April, 2020: Twenty years on from their divorce, Moore and Willis quarantine together with their daughters in LA as the coronavirus pandemic sweeps the globe. Tallulah and Scout’s boyfriends are also in lockdown with the family in LA.

Surprisingly, Willis’ second wife, Heming, and their daughters are in isolation at another residence, causing many to wonder if the famous exes have reconciled. But Heming — who in March celebrated her 11th wedding anniversary with Willis — quashes the rumours on Instagram.

“[Family bonding] at its finest, love and miss you guys,” she comments on the photo Moore posts.

It’s all love here in the extended Willis clan!
